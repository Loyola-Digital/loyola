### Overview: The 5 Scaling Frameworks

These are the frameworks Leila uses to scale teams to $100 million and beyond. They are minimally edited from her original internal training documents -- given to teams exactly as-is.

| # | Framework | Problem It Solves | When It Breaks |
|---|-----------|-------------------|----------------|

### Framework 1: Five Star Service Framework

**Why This Matters:**
- Customers are MORE likely to leave due to BAD SERVICE than due to price or product (McKinsey & Co study)
- Every single interaction either strengthens or weakens your brand
- Acquisition.com's average NPS is 88 (above Chick-Fil-A at 58 and Apple at 61)

**The 5 Service Standards:**

#### 1. Concern Star -- Show we care
- Thank customer for reaching out + personalize
- Give reassurance: "Not to worry, I am more than happy to assist you in resolving [issue] today!"
- Verify the issue for accuracy: "I understand you are struggling with [issue], is that correct?"
- At end, verify resolution was reached: "Today I assisted you with [issue] by helping you [steps taken]. Was I able to fully resolve your issues?"

#### 2. Courtesy Star -- Listen to customers
- **Active Listening:** Rephrase customer response to ensure understanding: "So it sounds like you are upset because..."
- **Probing Questions:** Find root issue, not surface complaint
- **Build Rapport:** Call by name, ask about their day, use casual language
- **Professional Tone:** Say please when asking, say thank you when info provided, acknowledge gratitude

#### 3. Educate & Empower Star
- **Answer first:** Give solution/answer immediately ("Here is the phone number and email you signed up with")
- **Educate on HOW:** Show where and how you found the answer ("Here is a link that shows where you can find this next time")
- **Empower going forward:** Teach what caused the issue and how to avoid it in future

#### 4. One & Done Star -- Resolve first time, every time
- **No leftovers** in customer service
- Verify issue was resolved + summarize steps taken
- Educate customer on self-resolution
- Offer additional assistance before wrapping up: "Is there anything else I can do to help you today?"

#### 5. Timeliness Star
- Respond immediately even if you can't resolve yet: "I will be with you in two moments so I can ensure you have my undivided attention"
- Resolve current issue before opening another
- Customers should be IMPRESSED, not just satisfied, with speed

**How To Measure: Post-Interaction Survey (5 questions, 60 seconds)**

Each dimension rated 1-5:
1. **Concern:** "Did the representative demonstrate empathy and genuine care?"
2. **Courtesy:** "Was the representative polite, respectful, and professional? Did they listen actively?"
3. **Educate & Empower:** "Did the representative provide accurate, helpful information and guidance?"
4. **One & Done:** "Was your issue resolved in a single interaction?"
5. **Timeliness:** "How quickly was your issue resolved?"
6. **Overall Five Star:** "Did the representative deliver a five-star experience?"

**Deployment:** Plug into email follow-up, CRM, CS platform, or automation tool.

### Framework 2: Gametape Review Framework

**Why This Matters:**
- One of the most beneficial calls you can implement in a company
- Has completely turned around marketing, sales, and CS departments in multiple portfolio companies
- Most departments operate in silos, but customers do NOT
- As CEO of a $500M portfolio, Leila STILL attends these calls

**What It Is:**
A weekly meeting where stakeholders from Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success observe a sales or delivery call together. Each person watches with a different objective.

**The 5-Step Process: The 5 A's**

#### Step 1: ARRANGE
- Recurring weekly meeting, 60-90 minutes
- **Host:** Head of Sales
- **Attendees:** Head of Marketing, Sales, Customer Success
- Add more people from struggling teams if needed
- No rigid rules on who attends -- add whoever will benefit

#### Step 2: ASSEMBLE
- 1 day prior, Head of Sales selects either:
  - A sales call
  - An onboarding call
  - A service call
- **Rotation rule:** If nothing is broken, rotate through call types in order
- **Focus rule:** If one department has an issue, spend 4 sequential weeks ONLY reviewing calls from that department

#### Step 3: ASSESS
- Play the call at 1.5x speed (host shares screen)
- Each stakeholder watches with different objectives:

| Role | What They Watch For |
|------|-------------------|
| **Sales Leader** | 1) What went right? 2) What should we improve? |
| **Marketing Leader** | 1) What objections could I overcome ahead of the call in marketing? 2) What objections can't be overcome in marketing that sales should train on? |
| **CS Leader** | 1) What was said that I can ensure gets handed off to delivery? 2) What was said that we can't deliver on, so sales should update their script? |

#### Step 4: ANALYZE
- All parties share feedback
- Determine **ONE thing** to commit to acting upon
- **"Pick the one thing that is the 'big domino' because if you pick too many things at once, even if they are objectively better, you might mess stuff up because your team doesn't have the bandwidth to see out all the changes with great execution."**

#### Step 5: ACT
- Document the action item and key points
- Monitor progress and adjust in future reviews

**Tracking Template:**

| | Sales (Assess) | Marketing (Assess) | CS (Assess) |
|---|----------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Highlights 1 | | | |
| Highlights 2 | | | |
| Highlights 3 | | | |
| Big Dominoes 1 | | | |
| Big Dominoes 2 | | | |
| Big Dominoes 3 | | | |

### Framework 3: High Performance Communication Framework

**Why This Matters:**
- Communication was a greater predictor of success than individual intelligence or skill level of the team (MIT study)
- The two best predictors were: team's ENERGY and ENGAGEMENT with each other outside formal meetings
- Together those two factors explained **40% of the variance in dollar productivity** among groups
- Bad communicators: nonresponsive, slow, short, vague -- you can't even see past their horrific communication to assess performance
- Silent stars: shining performers who lack confidence and never speak up -- just as problematic

**The 3 Pillars:**

#### Pillar 1: Energy
The number and nature of interactions among team members (verbal or nonverbal).

**How to improve Energy:**
1. **Become a Fast Communicator** -- Speed of communication has closest correlation to energy. "Think about great communicators -- do you wait days for them to respond? I think not."
2. **If You See Something, Say Something** -- Leila has an EOD calendar block labeled "Praise" where she reaches out to anyone who did something cool that day. "Never let a good gesture go unnoticed."
3. **Vary How You Communicate** -- Best communicators don't have one way. They blow up Zoom chat, Slack you, send gifts, comment on social media. Encourage using different mediums to react to teammates.

#### Pillar 2: Engagement
The distribution of energy among team members. In a 3-person team: engagement = average energy between A-B, A-C, and B-C.

**Key insight:** "The more members of the team that speak up during a decision-making process, the more profitable/successful the decision ends up being. This means the more YOU speak up, the smarter your team as a whole becomes."

**How to improve Engagement:**
1. **Pull On Meetings** -- Privately chat quiet teammates on calls, encourage them to speak, praise them when they do. Feed them lines via private chat until they're comfortable on their own.
2. **Distributed Presenting** -- Each team member responsible for presenting one piece of the team meeting. Creates even distribution of talk time.
3. **Buddy Up** -- Strategically place non-interacting teammates on projects together. Improves communication AND reduces dependency on you.

#### Pillar 3: Exploration
Communication that teammates engage in OUTSIDE their core team (with other departments).

**Correlation:** High-performing teams collaborate more with other teammates and departments.

**How to improve Exploration:**
1. **Cross-Team Projects** -- Set up projects where different departments work together. "Do you NEED 2 departments? Maybe not, but you WILL benefit from it."
2. **Rotation Programs** -- Have teammates join projects or groups outside their usual role. Monthly cross-department attendance on calls.
3. **Casual Events** -- Monthly game nights, pizza parties, luncheons. Annual full-team meetups where seating arrangements and activities are purposely mixed so nobody interacts with their typical team for 2 days.

**How to Deploy:**
1. Share document with leaders, ask them to grade their teams 1-10 on Energy, Engagement, Exploration
2. Ask the TEAM how they want to take action (don't come up with all ideas yourself)
3. Focus on ONE area, no matter how bad the others are

### Framework 4: Monday Hour One Framework

**Why This Matters:**
- Provides transparency for management
- Ensures no one is overloaded or underloaded
- Allows proper work dispersion and project deadline management
- Allows managers to see exactly what everyone is working on, utilization, who needs help, and alignment -- at a glance

**What It Is:**
ONE hour (usually less) of planning that saves 10-12 hours the rest of the week. Pre-plan all working hours for the week. Manager should be able to look at calendar and see all time blocked and properly allocated.

**The 6-Step Process:**

#### Step 1: The Look Back
Review previous week's calendar:
- What did you NOT get done? What is unresolved?
- Review all sent emails -- what is not followed up on?
- What happened last week that needs to pull forward?
- Review communication platform -- anything missed or forgot to respond to?
- Review "constraints" from last week:
  - What process/communication/document can prevent that constraint from happening again?

#### Step 2: The Look Forward
Review next 1-3 months calendar:
- What do you need to start working on?
- Review project management tool -- what projects are upcoming?
- Review next month on calendar -- what needs preparation?
- Review quarterly goals
- Review EOW reports -- what steps need to be taken?

#### Step 3: Prioritize
- What is most important to move you/your team/the business forward?
- **"We do NOT schedule priorities around our other stuff. We schedule other stuff around our priorities."**

#### Step 4: Schedule Your Priorities
- Schedule projects/tasks/work blocks for priorities FIRST
- Delete or move anything that doesn't revolve around these
- Be realistic -- insert lunch, breaks, school pickup/dropoff
- **Be specific:** No "project work" blocks. Must be named to the specific project.
- Insert high-priority projects first, THEN schedule email, project management, Facebook, and small recurring tasks

#### Step 5: Schedule Overflow/Ad-Hoc Time
- Account for estimation errors (things take different time than expected)
- Set apart time for urgent ad-hoc requests (customer requests, follow-ups, items from managers)
- **"You would be surprised that if we put constraints on our time, we can typically get items done in much less time than if we give ourselves too much time."**

#### Step 6: Color Code Your Calendar
- **Blue:** Recurring meetings
- **Red:** Admin tasks
- **Yellow:** One-off meetings
- **Green:** Projects/Priorities
- **Purple:** Break/Food time
- **Orange:** Personal events/time

**Result:** Easy visual measurement of where time is spent. Full transparency for managers.

### Framework 5: Pay Increase Framework

**Why This Matters:**
- Companies without a pay philosophy lose good people through overpaying bad people or underpaying good ones
- No standard = unfair and unequal pay
- "People talk. They share what they get paid more than you would ever guess."
- Must be transparent so pay is never an area of contention
- Want to do this BEFORE you grow; if you haven't, you can't implement too soon

**The Key Principle:**
"The single most important factor in earning a raise is demonstrating consistent excellence in your work. High performers are rewarded the fastest."

**The 4 Performance Categories:**

| Category | Description | Raise Range |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Standard Performance | Regular performance, no exceptional results | 1-3% |
| Consistent Performance & Reliability | Consistently meets expectations, reliable contributor | 4-5% |
| Excellence / High Performance / Added Responsibilities | Exceeds expectations, takes on extra | 5-10% |
| Promotion / New Skills / Significant New Responsibilities | Recently promoted, acquired new skills, major new duties | 10-20% |

**Common Circumstances Leading to Bigger Raises:**
1. **Significant increase in responsibilities** -- substantial role change, bigger team, formally documented new tasks
2. **Promotion to management/leadership** -- overseeing a team
3. **Consistent performance and achievement** -- leading to tasks beyond original scope
- "We don't reward fantastic individual contributors any less than leaders -- both are crucial to company performance"

**Raise Frequency:**
- NOT on a predetermined schedule
- NOT distributed equally across company or departments
- Evaluated on individual, case-by-case basis
- General rule: no more than once per calendar year (unless promotion or special circumstance)

**How to Ask for a Raise (7 Steps):**

#### Step 1: Understand Your Value and Market Rate
- Research position's market value (Glassdoor, Payscale, Bureau of Labor Statistics)
- Assess unique contributions: completed projects, revenue generated, cost savings, process improvements
- **"Lead with data, not emotions"**

#### Step 2: Determine Appropriate Increase
- Know what percentage to ask for (within the Pay Raise Framework)
- Be ready with specific numbers: "Based on my research, I would like to request an increase of $5,000 to $7,000"

#### Step 3: Prepare for the Conversation
- Approach professionally and calmly
- Start with positive note about job satisfaction
- Sample: "I really enjoy working here. Over the past year, the scope of my work has expanded significantly, and my contributions have increased. I would like to discuss reviewing my compensation."

#### Step 4: Present a Strong Case
- Be specific and evidence-based
- Have supporting documentation ready (industry salary reports, achievement list)
- **"The best pay increase requests I have seen -- the employee has put together a slide deck presentation"**

#### Step 5: Practice Patience and Professionalism
- Give manager time (salary decisions require review and upper management discussions)
- Don't expect immediate response
- Acceptable to discuss salary once per year unless exceptional circumstances

#### Step 6: Avoid Over-Asking
- Asking too often = appears dissatisfied and uncommitted
- Annual reviews are the standard cadence

#### Step 7: Follow Up Respectfully
- Send polite email thanking manager for their time
- Reiterate commitment to role and company
- Gently remind them of the request

## Step-by-Step Processes

### Deploying Five Star Service
1. Learn the 5-star framework (Concern, Courtesy, Educate & Empower, One & Done, Timeliness)
2. Train your CS team on each standard with tactical scripts
3. Set up post-interaction survey (5 questions, 1-5 scale, <60 seconds)
4. Plug survey into email follow-up / CRM / CS platform
5. Measure and review scores regularly
6. Coach reps on lowest-scoring areas

### Deploying Gametape Review
1. Schedule recurring weekly 60-90 min meeting
2. Head of Sales hosts, Marketing + Sales + CS leads attend
3. Day before: select a sales, onboarding, or service call to review
4. Play call at 1.5x, each stakeholder takes notes on their specific objectives
5. Share feedback, commit to ONE action item (the big domino)
6. Document and monitor progress
7. Repeat weekly forever

### Deploying High Performance Communication
1. Share framework with leaders
2. Have leaders grade teams 1-10 on Energy, Engagement, Exploration
3. Ask teams how THEY want to improve (don't dictate all ideas)
4. Focus on ONE area at a time
5. Implement specific tactics (fast communication, praise blocks, buddy systems, cross-team projects)
6. Re-grade quarterly

### Deploying Monday Hour One
1. Block 1 hour every Monday morning (or weekend)
2. Look Back: Review previous week (uncompleted tasks, unsent follow-ups, unresolved constraints)
3. Look Forward: Review next 1-3 months (upcoming projects, quarterly goals)
4. Prioritize items (most important first)
5. Schedule priorities first, then everything else around them
6. Add overflow/ad-hoc time blocks
7. Color code calendar
8. Supervisor reviews calendar for transparency

### Deploying Pay Increase Framework
1. Define the 4 performance categories with percentage ranges
2. Share framework with all team members upon joining
3. Train managers on evaluation process
4. Make it transparent -- everyone knows what to expect
5. Evaluate raises individually (not on schedule, not equally distributed)
6. Document all raise decisions and reasoning

## Tactical Advice

### For Customer Service Teams
- Always respond immediately, even if you can't solve yet -- "I will be with you in two moments"
- Resolve one issue completely before opening another
- Summarize steps taken at the end of every interaction
- Show customers HOW you found the answer so they can self-serve next time
- Use the customer's name throughout the interaction

### For Cross-Department Alignment
- If one department is struggling, spend 4 consecutive weeks ONLY reviewing calls from that department in gametape sessions
- Marketing should be listening to sales calls to learn real objections to overcome in ads/content
- Sales should know what delivery can and cannot fulfill so they don't oversell
- CS should know what was promised in sales so handoff is seamless

### For Communication
- Have an EOD "praise" calendar block to recognize teammates daily
- Use private chat to coach quiet people into speaking up on calls (feed them lines)
- Assign each team member a piece to present in meetings (forced engagement)
- Mix up seating and activities at team events so people interact across departments
- "The more the team communicates, the less they need ME"

### For Time Management
- No generic "project work" blocks -- every block must name the specific project
- Schedule high-priority work FIRST, then fit in email/admin around it
- Add realistic breaks (lunch, school pickup, mental health walks)
- "You would be surprised that constraints on time help things get done faster"

### For Pay Management
- Set up the framework BEFORE you grow, not after
- People WILL share their salaries -- make sure your pay is defensible
- Reward individual contributors as much as leaders
- Best raise requests come with a slide deck presentation and market data
- One raise per year unless promotion or dramatic responsibility change
