## Core Frameworks

### 1. The Ad Kaleidoscope Framework

The most important tool Hormozi uses to run paid ads. It applies "more, better, new" specifically to advertising.

**Core Principle:** 80% of advertising results come from 20% of winners (Pareto/Power Law). If you make 100% of your ads like the top 20%, you get 5x the output.

**The 4-Step Process:**

```
Step 1: Identify Winners -> Step 2: Remix Winners (80% effort) -> Step 3: Remake Winners -> Step 4: Try New Ideas (20% effort)
                                                                                              ^
                                                                              Repeat when new winner found
```

1. **Identify Winners** -- Find ads that outperform (top 20%, 10%, 5%). The more ads you make, the more winners you have to choose from.

2. **Remix Winners (80% of effort)** -- Same raw footage, vary the edits. Post-production variations. Fastest to make but fatigue fastest. Buys time for step 3.

3. **Remake Winners** -- New ads with same winning script. Re-filming with real-world variable changes. Takes longer but can keep you fed for months/years.

4. **Try New Ideas (20% of effort)** -- Test totally new concepts. Keep running permutations of previous winners until a new winner emerges. Then put it through the Kaleidoscope again.

### 2. Remix Variations (Post-Production -- Computer Changes)

In order from easiest to hardest:

| Variation | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **New Speed** | Change playback to 1.1x or 1.2x | Faster/slower versions convert the same = top performers |
| **New Filters** | Black & white, sepia, higher contrast | Changes visual appearance without re-recording |
| **New Background/Border** | Change background color, add thin trim | Simple color swaps or border additions |
| **New Fonts/Captions** | Change text styling | Different font, size, or style in captions |
| **New Headline** | Replace text overlay with different copy | "Double Your Leads Fast" -> "Steal Our 30-Day Blueprint" |
| **New Medium** | Export video frame as static image | Take winning hook, make standalone image |
| **New Format** | Resize for different placements | Square, vertical, horizontal for each platform |
| **New Meat** | Splice in different middle section | Keep winning hook, swap body content |
| **New Effects** | Add motion graphics, stickers, animations | Focus effects on the hook (what most see first) |

### 3. Remake Variations (Pre-Production -- Real-World Changes)

In order from easiest to hardest:

| Variation | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **New Clone** | Same everything, just hit record again | Micro-differences in lighting/sound make it fresh |
| **New Props** | Swap objects, costumes, hair, accessories | Remove banana prop; add whiteboard |
| **New Examples** | Change names/numbers in same script | "$4,664/mo Kyle" -> "$6,398/mo Sarah" |
| **New Setting** | Move to different location | Studio -> office -> beach -> car |
| **New Talent** | Different person delivers same script | Match talent to target avatar demographics |
| **New Combination** | Multiple changes at once | New prop + new setting + new filter |

### 4. The Value Equation in Ads

Every ad should hit the four pillars of the value equation:

```
           Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = --------------------------------------------------------
           Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice
```

- **Dream Outcome** -- What the prospect craves
- **Perceived Likelihood** -- Proof it will work for them
- **Speed** -- How fast they'll see results
- **Ease** -- How simple the process is

### 5. The Two Functions of Good Ads

Good ads do exactly two things:
1. **Get the prospect's attention** (importance)
2. **Get the prospect to take action** (benefit > cost of acting)

### 6. The Emotional Avatar Testimonial Narrative Rhythm

```
PERSONAL STAKES -> PERSONAL PAIN -> PROFESSIONAL PAIN -> TURNING POINT -> WORK -> PROFESSIONAL GOOD -> PERSONAL GOOD -> ADDRESS SKEPTICISM -> REASON WHY -> CTA
```

## The 20 Best Ad Frameworks of All Time

### Section I: Gym Launch Ad Frameworks (5)

#### Framework #1: Personal Testimonial / Origin Story
**Why it works:** Functions as one gigantic open loop. Builds mystery while eliminating implied objections. Claims ridiculous outcome, shows circumstances opposite to what you'd expect, stacks "anti-proof" until snapping closed with massive payoff.

**Structure:**
1. Make a wild promise/claim in first 5 seconds (visually or verbally)
2. Show yourself in an obviously "wrong" environment
3. Walk the viewer through a rapid "problems tour" -- rattle off every objection
4. Expand the open loop, stack more negatives -- keep revealing reasons success shouldn't happen here
5. Snap the loop closed with overwhelming proof (cash, contracts, dashboard)
6. Deliver takeaway and soft CTA

**Key insight:** "If we can sell here, you can sell anywhere." The anti-proof eliminates all excuses.

#### Framework #2: Prop Comedy
**Why it works:** Entertaining, fast-moving, clear and to the point. Proves you know what you're doing through the quality of the ad itself.

**Structure:**
1. Map value-equation questions. Write 3-4 "Yes" prompts: dream outcome, speed, ease, proof
2. Assign one prop + one mini-setting per line
3. Film each line separately as individual clips
4. Use whip-cuts or camera swings between clips -- momentum drags viewers forward
5. Layer upbeat music with huge high-contrast captions
6. Finish with CTA card, freeze-frame 1 full second

**Critical tip:** The first setting must be one your avatar recognizes (gym for gym owners). Screenshare the page they'll land on to show what happens next.

#### Framework #3: Emotional Avatar Testimonial
**Why it works:** Birthed one of the best ads ever. 100+ variations all performed. Six points for the testimonial subject to hit:
1. What was life like before (personally)
2. What was life like before (professionally)
3. What skepticism did you have
4. Why did you take action anyway
5. What was life after (personally)
6. What was life after (professionally)

**Structure:**
1. Pick client, record in setting that communicates they're like the avatar
2. Show sad setting fast (cut within half second to empty/gloomy shot + emotional music)
3. Open with blunt pain statement: "We were two months from shutting our doors"
4. Stack 5-6 rapid-fire pains and stakes (low revenue, pregnancy, second job)
5. Mark clear turning point: "We joined [product]"
6. Describe dream outcome with numbers AND personal benefits
7. Highlight speed and ease: "Hired staff, stepped away in 4.5 months"
8. Insert brief honest disclaimer: "It's not easy, but it's completely worth it"
9. Give condensed "reason why" to take action
10. End with one crystal-clear CTA on screen + voice-over

#### Framework #4: Show Don't Tell
**Why it works:** The closer a prospect can approximate proof to their situation, the more compelling it is. No ad copy needed -- just showing the result clearly IS the ad.

**Structure:**
1. Show the outcome happening instead of saying it happened
2. Frame what they're looking at with fewest possible words (high-contrast color)
3. Get people to move in unison if possible (synchronized swimming effect -- we can't help but stare)
4. Non-verbal ads need non-verbal CTAs

**MASSIVE PRO TIP:** Many people moving at once draws attention. Many people moving at once IN UNISON is even more powerful. Think flash mobs, cheerleading, synchronized swimming. This stops the scroll better than narration ever can.

#### Framework #5: Long Term Result
**Why it works:** Contrasts results against "one trick ponies." Bigger results on longer timelines. Brings in absolute highest quality customers who pay more, stay longer, get better results, and refer friends.

**Critical lesson:** "Don't optimize for CAC, optimize for LTV:CAC. This type of ad doesn't optimize for cheap leads, it optimizes for big spenders."

**Structure:**
1. Open with visual matching avatar + wild before/after headline
2. Hook with single pain sentence
3. Stack 2-3 specific pains only advanced avatars relate to
4. Mark the decision point (name solution + timestamp for credibility)
5. Show Fast Win + Big Win (two tiers)
6. Add detailed metrics only advanced people understand (LTV, churn, margin)
7. Include life improvements and "soft" benefits
8. Reason-why CTA through peer endorsement

**Key insight:** "If your ad talks only to beginners, you never get the advanced guy. No one else will talk to advanced customers because they're afraid to ostracize anyone. So it's ripe for the taking."

### Section II: ALAN Ad Frameworks (5)

#### Framework #1: Looking For 5 Avatars
**Template:** "I'm looking for # AVATARS who are looking to DREAM OUTCOME."

One of the most classic direct response ads of all time. Works in any niche. Worked 70 years ago. Works today.

**Structure:**
1. Film in educational setting (whiteboard, digital screen)
2. Open with one-liner: "I'm looking for 5 [AVATAR] who want [OUTCOME] but don't want [PAIN] so I can [REASON WHY]. Here's my [PROOF]. If interested, [KEEP WATCHING]."
3. Explain why only taking limited clients (case studies, proving it works, just starting)
4. Why your thing is better than what's out there + proof
5. Split risk with customer (performance compensation)
6. CTA to assistant: "My EA will confirm you fit the requirements"

#### Framework #2: New vs. Old (ADucational)
**Why it works:** Educates prospect enough to make a decision. Builds authority. Works for complex/higher-ticket products. Both builds brand and drives sales.

**Structure:**
1. Film in educational setting, call out exact avatar in <5 seconds
2. Make big claim with details ("$1,500/mo clients to $15,000/mo -- same niche")
3. Attribute all pain to their outdated model (be specific with problems)
4. Reveal new way, anchor to simple metric
5. Drive home with case study or hypothetical
6. Single proof line reinforcing authority
7. Risk-reversed offer if possible
8. CTA previewing the process

#### Framework #3: Why You Will Never
**Why it works:** Capitalizes on understanding prospects' problems better than they do. Builds pressure with each specific statistic/problem until prospect thinks "he must be an insider."

**Structure:**
1. Stand in front of whiteboard, address exact avatar in first sentence
2. Establish authority with single giant credential
3. Rapid-fire through prospect-specific pains with exact numbers
4. Introduce new solution (name it something non-obvious to preserve curiosity)
5. CTA to lead magnet or VSL

**Key insight:** "You demonstrate your expertise by how specific or relevant you can describe the problem." A problem so specific someone says "he must really understand this space" or "I feel like he's describing my life right now."

#### Framework #4: The North Star
**Why it works:** Creates a single predictive metric, then shows prospects where they fall in a hierarchy of outcomes based on that metric.

**Structure:**
1. Direct callout + nightmare scenario
2. Establish authority (one sentence)
3. List hierarchy of outcomes based on the metric (under $5K LTV = never crack $300K/yr; $10K = $1M; $15K = $3M; $20K+ = $1M/month)
4. List specific pains at each level
5. Introduce how your method solves the north star metric
6. CTA to next step

**On Specificity:** Hormozi lists 8+ specific music-related problems that only a gym owner would know. "The narrower your focus, the more examples there are."

#### Framework #5: No BS Selfie
**Why it works:** Very short. Bang out tons with little effort. Different settings. Constraining time forces only the most important words. Sells the next step, not the whole thing.

**Structure:**
1. Let the setting do the selling (walk and talk with credibility signals)
2. Start with if-then pain statement
3. Introduce solution in one sentence (don't explain it)
4. Show proof someone worse than them got better results
5. Short CTA

**KEY LINE:** "We've helped kids in their twenties build $6 million-plus agencies." Age juxtaposition implies bigger/older agencies can do even better. Makes prospect think "if they could do it, so can I."

### Section III: Skool Ad Frameworks (5)

#### Framework #1: Trending Format
**Why it works:** Capitalizes on current popular video formats (e.g., podcast clips). "Organic is always ahead of paid ads because of volume and speed of testing. If you want to see where ads will be in 6-12 months, look at where organic is today."

**Structure:**
1. Record solo podcast clip that looks 100% organic
2. Lead with recent surprising discovery
3. Stack speed and scaled proof in one line
4. Hammer perceived likelihood and ease by removing every classic objection using proof
5. CTA

**Important:** Always include BOTH big and average outcomes. "People want to know the big thing is possible, but most importantly, want to understand what's likely."

#### Framework #2: Value Anchor
**Why it works:** Two back-to-back "stacks" that anchor high then price drop. Plus variable reward making it "better than free."

**Template:**
```
How much would you pay for DREAM OUTCOME? $XXX, $XX, $X?
-> Explain offer
-> Anchor value at marketplace rate
-> Give it free/less than anchor
-> Add last-minute incentive (better than free)
-> CTA
```

**Structure:**
1. Open with scroll-stopping money/prop visual (3-second keyframe, high-contrast backdrop)
2. Ask descending value questions: "Would you pay $1,000? $100? What if it were free?"
3. Put dream outcome on fast timeline
4. Make product valuable before giving it away (call out real competitor pricing)
5. Make it better than free with variable incentive

#### Framework #3: Bribe
**Why it works:** "Volume negates luck." Born from a 250-ad shoot in one day. Pure volume-and-testing. "There's zero magic here."

**Structure:**
1. Ask for contact info with pattern-interrupt: "Real quick, can I get your email?"
2. Tell them what they'll get: "$1,000 of free stuff straight to your inbox"
3. Explain why it's valuable by anchoring against real market pricing ($1K, $5K, $10K+)
4. Clarify dream outcome in one line
5. Give reason why + additional incentive
6. Make CTA mirror the hook

**Key insight:** "Only amateurs reinvent the wheel. Pros use proven playbooks and checklists and make up for the rest with volume."

#### Framework #4: Underdog
**Why it works:** Sets low expectations (more believable). People already doubt they'll succeed -- this squashes that objection upfront.

**Structure:**
1. Lead with modest, believable number ($4,664/mo -- the LOWEST on the leaderboard)
2. Stack proof with progressively larger results, then pivot BACK to smallest
3. Give good "reason why" this is a big deal
4. CTA

**Key insight:** "Don't be afraid to talk about your lower tier outcomes. Beat people to the punch. They're already doubtful."

**Marketing rule:** "They'll only think it's a big deal if you tell them it is. So tell them!"

#### Framework #5: Challenge With Prize
**Why it works:** Dynamite framework. Buckets of users. Hook + visuals combo dominated for the rest of the year.

**Structure:**
1. Give away something insane (match to what your audience values most)
2. Put it on a time constraint ("in the next 30 days")
3. Prove somebody else has won before
4. Tell them exactly how to win (one clear metric, one clear path)
5. Show how you'll help them win (tools, checklists, weekly Q&A)
6. Make NOT winning valuable too (free stuff is valuable on its own)
7. Show proof the free thing works
8. Show them what happens the moment they take the next step (screen recording of signup)

### Section IV: $100M Leads Book Launch Ad Frameworks (5)

#### Framework #1: Flying Prop
**Why it works:** Stuff that moves beats stuff that doesn't. Real stuff that moves beats computer stuff. Entire ad is curiosity-driven via daisy-chained open loops.

**Daisy-chain example:** Banana -> money spent on event -> birthday -> giving a secret away

**Structure:**
1. Catch flying prop on camera
2. Quick joke tying prop to offer
3. Pile on tangible reasons (time invested, money spent, relationship capital)
4. Hint at big secret, leave loop open
5. CTA

**Pro tip:** Keep a shed/storage closet full of props. "Anytime something catches your attention -- buy it -- it'll catch someone else's too."

#### Framework #2: Bribe (Book Launch Version)
**Why it works:** Same hook structure as Skool bribe ad -- proves frameworks work agnostic of industry.

**Structure:**
1. Tell them what you want (email, registration)
2. Tell them what they'll get
3. Explain why it's valuable (costs you incurred as proxy for their benefit)
4. Emphasize it's a big deal
5. Make free thing mysterious through comparisons: "More than a gift card but less than a Tesla"

**Key insight:** "Being vague allows people's imaginations to fill in the perfect copy for each of them. And you're not gonna beat anyone's imagination."

#### Framework #3: The Rumors Are True
**Why it works:** Leverages social proof + curiosity. Creates: 1) feeling of being left out, 2) feeling left out of something IMPORTANT.

**Structure:**
1. "The rumors are true -- my [product] is finally coming out"
2. Tell them what they'll get
3. Explain value through personal investment (hours, money, iterations)
4. Add curiosity (unnamed secret)
5. Visual CTA (end screen only, no spoken CTA -- experiment with this)

#### Framework #4: You're Being Lied To
**Why it works:** Classic old way vs. new way. Confrontational style paired with confrontational hook. Walking and talking works exceptionally well (rarer, so it stands out).

**Structure:**
1. Walk towards camera aggressively: "You're being lied to"
2. Expose old way ("If you build it, they will come -- that's NOT true")
3. Present new way ("If you TELL them, they will find you")
4. Show proof new way works
5. Share selfish reason why (people trust openly shared biases)
6. Promise fast results (frame time saved, not speed of outcome)
7. Tell them what to do next

#### Framework #5: Data Stack
**Why it works:** Numbers at the beginning of ads, especially big numbers, work every time. Half-written board invokes more curiosity.

**Structure:**
1. Face camera, point at half-written board: "This will blow your mind"
2. Collect and list most impressive stats rapid-fire
3. Tell them how many people have already shown interest (social proof)
4. Explain true limitations (scarcity)
5. Tell them what to do next (fast, clear, easy)

## Key Principles

### The Henry Ford Lesson
"You will tire of your advertising before your prospects even know your name. You make campaigns for them, not for you. When an ad finally works, keep running it until it STOPS working -- never until you get bored."

### Volume Negates Luck
"The process is simple: make lots of ads, spot the few that perform, pour 80% of effort into remixing and remaking those, spend the other 20% hunting for the next winner. One winner is enough to start the flywheel. Permutations keep it spinning."

### Winners Are Rare -- Squeeze Every Drop
"80% of sales come from 20% of ads. When an ad finally clicks, hammer it until it dies -- then rerun with Kaleidoscope variations."

### Frameworks Are Industry-Agnostic
"These ad structures work agnostic of industry. Every hook, proof stack, and CTA can sell whatever you've got."

### Organic Predicts Paid
"Organic is always ahead of paid ads because of the volume and speed of testing for creators. If you want to see where ads will be in 6-12 months, just look at where organic is today."

### Optimize for LTV:CAC, Not CAC
"Don't optimize for CAC, optimize for LTV:CAC. You can get $100 customers who pay you $200, or $1,000 customers who pay you $10,000. They'll cost more but you'll make more."

### Specificity Is Everything
"You demonstrate your expertise by how specific you can describe the problem. A problem so specific that someone hears it and says 'he must be an insider.'"

### The Avatar-First Visual Principle
Your ideal customer should immediately see the background, clothing, and spokesperson as "like them." If targeting gym owners, film IN a gym. If targeting agency owners, film in front of a whiteboard. "It's so simple, and yet so few people ever do it."

### Two Types of Proof
1. **Big outcomes** -- what's possible ($43K/month in recurring revenue)
2. **Average/modest outcomes** -- what's likely ($4,664/month -- the lowest on the leaderboard)
Always include both. "People want to know the big thing is possible, but most importantly, want to understand what's likely."

### Damaging Admissions Build Trust
Include honest disclaimers in ads: "It's not easy, but it's completely worth it." The admission boosts credibility and decreases skepticism.

```
80% of effort -> Remixes + Remakes of winners
20% of effort -> Testing new ideas

```
Attention (hook) + Action (offer + CTA) = Good Ad
```

### The Proof Proximity Principle
```
Proximity of proof to prospect's situation = Persuasion power
Same person + same city + same business + visible results >> Different person + different country + verbal claim

## Step-by-Step Processes

### How To Run The Ad Kaleidoscope

1. Make as many ads as you can until you find a winner
2. Run winner through Kaleidoscope -- start with Remixes (fastest)
3. Simultaneously begin Remakes (re-film with same script)
4. 80% energy on Kaleidoscope variations, 20% on totally new ads
5. When new winner emerges from the 20%, put it through Kaleidoscope
6. Never stop previous winners -- just add more winners to the rotation
7. Repeat forever

Ask your customer to hit these 6 points:
1. What was life like BEFORE (personally)?
2. What was life like BEFORE (professionally)?
3. What SKEPTICISM did you have?
4. Why did you TAKE ACTION anyway?
5. What was life AFTER (personally)?

## Tactical Advice

### On Ad Hooks
- Numbers at the beginning of ads (especially big numbers) work every time
- Half-written boards invoke curiosity (people want to see it completed)
- Props that catch your attention will catch others' too -- keep a storage closet of interesting objects
- Walking and talking almost always outperforms standing still

### On Proof
- The closer proof approximates the prospect's situation, the lower the perceived risk
- Non-verbal proof (showing packed gym) often beats verbal claims
- Timestamps increase credibility massively ("We joined in March 2023")
- Non-verbal clips get more organic reach

### On Targeting Advanced Customers
- Talk about things only advanced customers care about (LTV, churn, margins)
- Use language only the upper echelon would understand
- "No one else will talk to the advanced customer because they're afraid to ostracize anyone. So it's ripe for the taking."
- You'll get both the upper market AND the smaller market

### On Hook Recycling
- Reuse hooks across industries and businesses
- The "Bribe" hook appeared in both Skool and Book Launch -- different products, both crushed
- Once you find a winning hook, make permutations into static form too (images from video hooks)

### On Production
- Ads don't need to be fancy, they need to get clicks

### Hormozi Writing Guidelines (from Affiliate materials)

1. **Below 3rd grade reading level**
2. **Present voice** (not progressive)
3. **Active not passive**
4. **Avoid adverbs** (they usually mean weak verbs)
5. **Simple/short sentences** (usually one comma max)
6. **Positive language** ("Keep Going" not "Don't Stop")
7. **Remove redundant words**
8. **Remove unnecessary words**
