## 1. Branding Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Make Anyone or Anything Famous on Purpose

### Core Frameworks

**Brand = Pairing.** Branding means pairing two or more things together. Good branding pairs your business with something the majority of your ideal audience likes. Bad branding pairs with something they dislike. No branding means you are invisible.

**Three Brand Variables:**
1. **Reach** -- how many people know about your stuff (advertising output)
2. **Influence** -- the chance it changes behavior when they see it
3. **Direction** -- whether behavior changes towards (buy) or away (avoid)

**Branding vs. Advertising:**
- Advertising = letting people know about your stuff
- Branding = what you let them know in the advertisement
- You can have great advertising + poor branding, or any combination

**Brand Measurement Matrix (8 positions):**
- High/Low Reach x High/Low Influence x Towards/Away
- A single brand occupies different positions depending on who you ask

**Levels of Authority (The Branding Cycle):**
1. What We Say (least influential) -- content, ads, outreach
2. What Other People Say -- affiliates, other customers
3. What They Experience Themselves (most influential) -- governs long-term brand

**The Bouquet Metaphor:** Think of branding like assembling a bouquet of flowers. Products, values, experiences, people are individual flowers. Tying them together forms the brand. What you include, from where, and how much influences the brand you create.

### Key Principles

- "You only get one name. Invest accordingly."
- Branding increases business value in all 3 ways: (1) more customers, (2) customers worth more, (3) keeps happening into the future
- Good branding: Lower CAC, raise LTV, lower risk (loyalty)
- Branded returns on advertising are absurd -- they just take time (why no one does it)
- "Any publicity is better than no publicity, but good publicity is better than bad."
- You do NOT need to be polarizing. Taylor Swift, Apple, Mother Teresa = strong, non-polar brands
- Your advertising has stronger influence on brand short-term; your product has stronger influence long-term
- Brand pivots: you will lose some audience. Just make sure you gain more than you lose
- Mistakes: overwhelm with good pairings until the bad one shrinks into irrelevance

**Branded vs. Unbranded comparison (same $2,000 ad spend):**
- Unbranded: 1% CTR, 4% conversion, 2 sales, $8K profit, 4:1 ROAS
- Branded: 3% CTR, 6% conversion, 18 sales, $90K profit, 45:1 ROAS

### Tactical Actions (4-Step Brand Building Process)

**Step 1: Figure Out Who Your Ideal Customer Is**
- Growing market
- Have enough money (can afford your stuff)
- Easy to target/find
- In pain (desperate need)

**Step 2: Figure Out What They Like**
- List their desires, problems, aspirations
- What outcomes do they want? What do they struggle with?

**Step 3: Associate Your Brand With Relevant Things They Like**
- Make content geared towards the audience
- Distribute it
- Iterate based on audience response
- Transfer influence/reach of pairings into the product itself

**Step 4: Optimize Associations For Max Profit**
5 directions to expand:
1. Up Market -- larger customers
2. Down Market -- smaller customers
3. Adjacent -- similar but slightly different market
4. Broader -- entire category
5. Narrower -- a slice of existing market

**Patience:** Branding has higher returns than direct response if measured over longer periods. Repeat your message every day -- new people find you daily.

### Checklist
- Figure out ideal avatar (growing, in pain, can afford, easy to find)
- Figure out what they like
- Associate through content, products, people they like
- Grow influence strategically in 5 directions
- Do more advertising to grow reach
- Make products match promises (virtuous branding cycle)
- When mistakes happen, double down on what they like
- Wait five years and don't give up

## 2. Fast Cash Playbook

**Subtitle:** The Fastest Way to Rip Cash Out of a Business

### Core Frameworks

**Fast Cash Plays:** Limited time offers advertised to your warmest audiences (current customers, previous customers, engaged leads). Run once per quarter. Different from nurturing new leads or long-term value-adds.

**10x the 10% Rule:** If you get 10% of your customers to pay 10x the price, you double your revenue. Since the customer is already acquired, extra revenue (minus delivery) drops straight to the bottom line. Can 2x-4x revenue from one out of ten customers.

**Fast Cash Play Architecture:**
- Scarcity (limited spots)
- Exclusivity (high touch)
- Big ticket (premium price)
- Urgency (limited time)

**Two Selling Modes:**
1. Push to Consult Sequence (7 days, books appointments)
2. Push to Automated Checkout Sequence (5 days, stampede launch)

### Key Principles

- "Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality."
- Fast Cash Plays: ~15% of annual revenue but nearly half of profit
- Despite being a smaller % of revenue, can be a much larger % of profit
- Warmest audiences buy most easily and cost nothing to reach
- 1 out of 9 Americans is a millionaire -- your customers CAN afford more
- Run every 90 days for optimal results
- "You will work more for these customers. You will earn that money."

### Tactical Actions

**What to Sell (Ultra-Premium Value Elements):**
- Attention: 1-on-1 or many-on-1 from senior people
- Personalization: customized solutions
- Convenience: 24/7 support, extended hours
- Status: special perks, badges, recognition
- Duration: longer than standard
- Speed: first in line, priority, fast response
- Experience: white glove, personal cell, dinners
- Access: in-person, retreats, behind the scenes
- Network: connection with other top customers
- Secrets: exclusive bonuses, trade secrets, vendor access

**Pricing:** 10x to 50x your current price. Go beyond comparison to other products. The value should shock them as much as the price.

**Offer Structure:** 1-3 core components + 3 bonuses (revealed sequentially through the campaign).

**How Many:** Cap at 5-10% of customers. Sell out fast to make next one more compelling.

**Push to Consult Sequence:**
- Day 7: Teaser text
- Day 6: Announcement email + text (core offer)
- Day 5: Recap + Bonus 1
- Day 3: Sales update (how many sold) + text
- Day 2: X spots left + Bonus 2 + text
- Day 1 AM: Warning + best bonus + text
- Day 1 PM: Sold out + text
- Optional: Back out notification

**Push to Automated Checkout Sequence:**
- Day 5: Primer text
- Day 4: Announcement email + text
- Day 2: Cart opens in 48hrs + text
- Day 1: Bonus 1 + set alarm + text
- 60 min out: text reminder
- 5 min: Cart open + best bonus + text
- 60 min post: Sales update + text
- 6 hours post: X spots left + hidden bonus + text
- 12 hours post: Sold out + text

**ROI Example (gym with $25K/mo recurring):**
- Q1: 10 x $6K = $60K
- Q2: 13 x $5K = $65K
- Q3: 8 x $6K = $48K
- Q4: 14 x $4K = $56K
- Total extra: $229K. Net income goes from $75K to $281K (3.75x)

**5 Reasons to Run Every 90 Days:**
1. Cleans pipeline of on-the-fence leads
2. Increases customer value
3. Shows something new/interesting
4. Allows delivery/reset cadence
5. All additional revenue drops to bottom line

### Checklist
- Assemble list (past + present customers)
- Pick offer (max out unscalable value)
- Pick price (10x-50x, be bold)
- Choose selling mode (consults vs automated checkout)
- Prep emails + texts with CTAs
- Send sequence, collect cash
- Deliver the stuff
- Collect 4Rs: Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells
- Cooldown list, provide value
- Set next date, rinse and repeat

## 3. GOATed Ads Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Make Ads That Get the Right People to Click

### Core Frameworks

**Ad Assembly Line Process:** Instead of making whole ads, assemble them from parts:
- 50 Hooks x 3-5 Meats x 1-3 CTAs = 150-750 ads per week
- Prep time split: 80% Hooks, 20% Meat, ~0% CTAs

**Eugene Schwartz Awareness Pyramid (adapted to continuous model):**
1. Most Aware -- knows your product, needs the deal
2. Product-Aware -- knows what you sell, unsure if right for them
3. Solution-Aware -- knows desired result, doesn't know your product
4. Problem-Aware -- senses problem, doesn't know solutions exist
5. Completely Unaware -- doesn't know they have a problem

**Key insight:** You haven't saturated the market. You've hit a wall with ad quality. Better ads = bigger audience converted. Make more ads, way more.

### Key Principles

- "You're gonna take at least five and change it to at least 500."
- 90% of ad work is preparation (research), not recording
- The better your ads, the bigger the audience they convert
- If 90% of hooks land in "Most Aware" bucket, spread them out broader
- When in doubt, go a little broader -- still catch warm + attract cold
- Don't get bored repeating -- it's the first time new customers see it
- "Clear > Clever" for CTAs

### Tactical Actions

**Hook Sources (5 places to find winning hooks):**
1. Winning hooks from your previous ads
2. Winning hooks from your free content (cross-platform)
3. Winning hooks from other people's ads
4. Winning hooks from other people's free content
5. Platform-specific ad libraries (last resort, inspiration only)

**Hooks by Awareness Level:**
- Most Aware: offer-driven ("50% off our best-selling XYZ")
- Product-Aware: proof-driven ("Discover why 10,000 people chose XYZ")
- Solution-Aware: promise-driven ("The fastest way to [achieve result]")
- Problem-Aware: pain-driven ("Tired of [specific problem]?")
- Completely Unaware: curiosity-driven ("The hidden danger in your daily routine")

**5 Ad Meat Formats:**
1. Demonstration Ads -- live use, reactions, unboxing, comparisons, before/after, high-production hero ads
2. Testimonial Ads -- UGC, direct to camera, podcast style, professional, raw iPhone, walk n talk, group/parade of proof, lifecycle, man-on-the-street, influencer collabs
3. Education Ads -- explainer videos, how-to listicles, whiteboard, high-performing organic
4. Story Ads -- storytelling, lifestyle, warnings/opportunities, documentary, skits, brand manifestos
5. Faceless Ads -- screenshots, text only, slide shows, animations, cartoon, visual effects

**CTA Formula (show AND tell):**
1. What to do
2. How to do it
3. When to do it
4. What they get for doing it
5. What happens next (optional but powerful)
- Demonstrate visually what clicking/following through looks like
- Add urgency, scarcity, guarantees, bonuses

**Scaling:** Find winners by making lots of variations. Double down on what works. Reuse winning hooks to make even more winners.

**Pro Tip:** Memes or meme-like content attract the largest percentage of audience. Culture-specific memes for narrower groups work like moths to flames.

## 4. Hooks Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Double Your Marketing in Under 5 Seconds

### Core Frameworks

**Hook = whatever gets the attention of your audience.** First few seconds of video, first thing heard, first line or image seen.

**Hook Anatomy (2 parts):**
1. Call Out -- gets prospect to say "This is for me" (verbal + nonverbal)
2. Condition for Value -- the hook itself, promising value if consumed

**The Cocktail Party Effect:** Even with tons of noise, a single relevant thing catches and holds attention. Your name at a party. Goal: harness this to cut through noise.

**70-20-10 Innovation Rule (from Google):**
- 70% Core: hooks proven in your own or others' content ("more of what works")
- 20% Emerging: "winner adjacent" -- promising but less proven, modeled from other niches
- 10% Big New Ideas: totally new, risky, could lead to huge wins

**Hook Types -- Verbal:**
- Labels: "*Local business owners, I have a gift for you*"
- Questions: yes questions, open questions
- Conditionals: scenarios leading to result/learning/command
- Commands: direct instructions
- Statements: declarative claims
- Lists/Steps: numbered items
- Narratives: stories and anecdotes
- Exclamations/Provocative Statements

**Hook Types -- Nonverbal:**
- Visual elements, sounds, movements
- Both verbal + nonverbal together is most powerful

### Key Principles

- "After you've written your headline, you've spent eighty cents of your advertising dollar." -- David Ogilvy
- If nobody notices your ad, nobody buys. Everything hinges on the first few seconds.
- Mastering hooks can 5x, 10x, 100x leads for the same effort
- If you have 10 hours to advertise, spend the first 8 on hooks
- For ads: record 10 hooks for every 1 piece of ad content
- For emails: run active split tests on every campaign, document winners
- "Let the data do the teaching" -- top 10% tells you what works, bottom 10% what doesn't

**Dean Graziosi / Larry King Story:** Changed ONLY the opening sentence (hook) of a Larry King infomercial. First version bombed. New hook: "Have you ever looked in the mirror and thought 'I should be further ahead by now?'" Same book, same price, same offer. Became a monster hit selling millions of books. One sentence changed everything.

### Tactical Actions

**Hook Creation Process:**
1. Create spreadsheet with tabs per platform (name, hook, views, link)
2. Review best hooks of all time before making content
3. 70% from proven winners (your own + others')
4. 20% from promising "winner adjacent" ideas
5. 10% completely new experiments
6. Document wins and losses for future reference

**Testing Hooks:**
- Ads: 10 hooks per 1 piece of ad content
- Short content: winning tweets become short hooks
- Long content: 3-4 hooks, team picks best in post-production
- Emails: active split tests on every campaign

### 121 Best Hooks (samples by category)

**Top Ad Hooks:**
- "Real quick question... Can I have your email address?"
- "You might be wondering why I just caught a banana..."
- "Would you pay $1,000 to have the business of your dreams in 30 days?"
- "Local business owners, I have a gift for you"
- "Read this if you're tired of being broke"

**Top Content Hooks (YouTube):**
- "You guys want to hear something completely insane"
- "In this video I'm going to talk to you about the 28 ways to stay poor"
- "One in every 250 businesses does over 10 million dollars a year"
- "This is my most brutally honest advice to my younger self"

**Top IG Hooks:**
- "If you wanna become obscenely wealthy..."
- "Poor people stay poor because they're afraid of other poor people judging them"
- "The most miserable place in business is $1-3 million. It's the swamp."
- "My first nine businesses didn't really amount to anything. Nine."

**Top Email Hooks:**
- "SHHHHH It's a SURPRISE!!"
- "Btw... (I have a favor to ask)"
- "Only open this if you have a business and want to scale"
- "We made a boo boo"

**Top Twitter Hooks:**
- "Everyone wants the view from the top, but no one wants the climb."
- "At first, you avoid hard fights. Then, you get used to hard fights. Finally, you start looking for hard fights."
- "Youth. Free time. Money. Pick two."

### Hook Checklist
- First time: look at top-performing ads/content, isolate hooks, write 50, use all
- Every time after: 70% from top 5 winners, 20% permutations, 10% completely different
- Good hooks contain: attention-catching call out + implication of value
- Create variety using: conditions, labels, questions, commands, statements, lists/steps, stories

## 5. Lead Nurture Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Get More Leads to Respond, Schedule, and Show

### Core Frameworks

**Four Pillars of Lead Nurture:**
1. **Availability** -- number of open appointment slots
2. **Speed** -- how fast you respond + how far out they schedule
3. **Personalization** -- making communication useful and relevant
4. **Volume** -- number of times you reach out before giving up

**Key Definitions:**
- Schedule Rate = % of engaged leads who schedule
- Show Rate = % of scheduled leads that show up
- Throughput = total % of engaged leads that show (schedule rate x show rate)

**BAMFAM:** "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting" -- NEVER end a call without scheduling the next one. "If you can't schedule with them right then, you certainly won't after putting them in limbo."

### Key Principles

- "If they do not show, they cannot buy."
- "Volume negates luck."
- "Money loves speed. Wealth loves time. Poverty loves indecision."
- 391% increase in sales when leads contacted within 60 seconds (Velocify)
- 78% of customers buy from the company that responds FIRST (Lead Connect)
- 44% of salespeople give up after the first attempt
- Average company takes 42 HOURS to respond to leads
- 20-40% increase in revenue = 2x-3x profit at 20% margins
- "There are no silver bullets, only golden BBs."
- "Yellows are the new gold" -- less qualified leads are still opportunities

### Tactical Actions

**Pillar I: Availability Tactics**
1. Take appointments 7 days/week (40% more availability)
2. Extend hours (6am-6pm PST covers most US sales)
3. Give 4 scheduling options per hour (every 15 min)
4. Offer inbound, outbound, AND self-scheduling options
5. Online scheduler: confirm right place, obvious times, eliminate steps

**Pillar II: Speed Tactics**
1. Speed to First Contact: respond in under 5 minutes (under 60 seconds = gold standard)
   - Litmus test: if you don't hear "Man that was fast!" at least once daily, you're too slow
2. Speed to First Appointment: limit scheduling to 3 days out max
   - Same-day show rates are highest
   - "I'm talking to you right now" show rate = 100%
   - Script to pull appointments forward included
3. Speed of Response: respond to all messages quickly between scheduling and appointment
   - "Hot handoff" to closer when lead is qualified and ready now

**Pillar III: Personalization Tactics**
1. Use lead's preferred communication -- reach out everywhere, continue where they respond
2. Qualify leads -- actively cancel bad appointments, focus time on good ones
3. Best leads to best closers -- score leads 1-5 or red/yellow/green, route accordingly
4. Segment messaging -- research leads before contact (easy) or build sequences per segment (hard)
5. Incentivize showing up:
   - Push incentive: give gift card before (reciprocity)
   - Pull incentive: A/B choice that assumes attendance ("black or pink shirt?")
6. Demonstrate proof -- match testimonials to lead demographics

**Pillar IV: Volume Tactics**
1. Scheduling cadence:
   - Call within 5 min of opt-in
   - Double-dial immediately
   - Leave voicemail, then text
   - Double dial + text 2 more times that day
   - Call 2x/day for next 2 days
   - Call + text 1x/day for next 4 days
   - Transition to long-term nurture after week 1
2. Automated reminders: immediately, 24hr, 12hr, 3hr before
3. Manual reminders from real phone: night before, morning of, 60min prior
4. BAMFAM: always book next meeting during current meeting

**Execution Culture:**
- Track and rank: show rate, close rate, lead-to-close ratio by rep
- "Be a garbage man" -- take all the leads others won't
- "Every sale is an opportunity for free practice"
- Commission bump for show-up rates (5-30% of sale value)
- Attention, affection, approval for top performers

### Lead Nurture Checklist
- Availability: 7 days, extended hours, 15-min slots, self-scheduling
- Speed: under 5 min first contact, book within 72hr, respond fast
- Personalization: multi-channel, qualify, segment, best-to-best, incentivize, proof
- Volume: 7+ reach-outs, automated + manual reminders, BAMFAM
- Execution: track metrics, build culture, love the boring work

## 6. Lifetime Value Playbook

**Subtitle:** Eight Ways to Get Customers to Buy More Stuff

### Core Frameworks

**The Crazy Eight -- 8 Ways to Increase LTV:**
1. Increase prices
2. Decrease costs (of delivering)
3. Increase # of purchases (recurring, decrease churn, follow up)
4. Cross-sell something different
5. Sell more (increase quantity -- bulk, more often, bigger)
6. Sell better (increase quality -- premium version)
7. Downsell fewer (lower quantity -- rather than nothing)
8. Downsell lower quality (rather than nothing)

**LTV Formula:**
- LTV = Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)
- Gross Profit = Revenue - Cost of Goods/Delivery
- For products: Gross Profit x Avg # Transactions = LTGP
- For recurring: Gross Profit / Churn % = LTGP

**Churn Calculation:**
- Churn = customers who left / original pool
- New signups do NOT affect churn calculation
- Example: 100 customers, lose 5 = 5% churn. LTV at $2,400 GP / 5% = $48,000

### Key Principles

- "The business owner who can make a customer more valuable to his business than to his competition wins."
- LTV is the fuel that powers advertising. Outspend competitors = get all customers.
- Cost of getting customers can only hit zero, but LTV can go infinitely high.
- "If customers like your stuff, they want to buy more stuff like it!"
- Favorite upsell: "More of -- or more help with -- what they just bought, but with faster results, less risk, less effort, less hassle -- for more money."
- Test prices every quarter (Profitwell study: tight relationship between profitability and frequency of pricing tests)
- Start low then go up: nudge price by 20% every 10 sales until dramatic drop

### Tactical Actions

**#1 Increase Prices:**
- Formula: Sales conversion rate x LTGP = optimal price
- Test every quarter
- If 10% profit, raise prices 20%, keep sales same = TRIPLE profit (not 20% growth)
- Start low, nudge 20% per 10 sales until dramatic drop

**#2 Decrease Costs:**
- Increase employee-to-customer ratio
- Offshore talent (80%+ cost cuts)
- Productize delivery (templates, automation)
- Done-for-you to done-with-you
- Cap usage
- Lifetime to annual access
- In-person to remote
- Cut meeting times (delete all recurring, add back only needed)
- Buy in bulk and prepay (10-20% guaranteed ROI)

**#3 Increase # of Purchases:**
- Add recurring (subscription/membership)
- Decrease churn (see Retention Playbook)
- Follow up with quarterly promotions (reactivation)

**#4 Cross-Sell Something Different:**
- Complements the first product
- LTV impact: conversion rate x gross profit of upsell added to original LTV
- Must not dramatically change who you serve or what you do daily

**#5 Sell More (Quantity Upsell):**
- Bulk purchasing (prepay for year)
- Increased frequency (monthly to every 3 weeks)
- Bigger package (1 hour to 3 hours)
- Offer quantity upsell FIRST, then downsell standard. 20%+ lifts.

**#6 Sell Better (Quality Upsell):**
Premium version toggles:
- Faster response times, more availability (days/hours/locations)
- Better service ratio (1-on-1 vs group)
- Better communication method
- Higher provider qualifications
- Live vs recorded, in-person vs remote
- DIY to DWY to DFY
- Personalization level

**#7 Downsell Fewer (Quantity):**
- Fewer units, smaller version, less frequent
- Only offer to people who do NOT qualify for main offer
- This-or-nothing: this beats nothing
- Example: 50% more revenue per visitor with downsell added

**#8 Downsell Lower Quality:**
- Same result but: slower, less availability, more junior staff, less personalization, worse guarantee
- Only for unqualified prospects
- Little operational drag since you already do/make the stuff

## 7. Marketing Machine Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Turn Your Business Into an Ads Machine

### Core Frameworks

**The Marketing Machine:** A systematic checklist where every item represents a reliable stream of customer-generated videos, images, and words for advertising. The bottom of the business (sales, support, events) feeds the top (ads, content, outreach).

**Key insight:** 40 out of 50 top-performing ads at Gym Launch did NOT have Hormozi's face. 80% were customer-generated. This made the business sellable for $46.2M.

**7 Marketing Machine Nodes:**
1. Lifecycle Ads
2. Social Media Scrape
3. Events (stage ads, pro testimonials, man-in-the-street)
4. Communication & Communities Scrape
5. Bonuses, Milestones & Awards
6. Competitions
7. Delivery moments

**6-Point Testimonial Script:**
1. Internal struggle -- rock bottom before, what they hated
2. External struggle -- objective metrics before
3. Skepticism -- main concerns about buying/joining
4. Overcome -- what made them do it anyway
5. External victory -- objective metrics now
6. Internal victory -- best moment since, what they can do now

### Key Principles

- "If I got hit by a bus today, what would keep the business growing tomorrow?"
- A company relying on your face is always less valuable
- "It's never really been about you... it's about them. Put them in the spotlight."
- Customer-generated ads get fresh creative delivered to your inbox every week
- "One > None" -- start with one process, add more over time
- "We've never made less by giving more."

### Tactical Actions

**Lifecycle Ads:**
- Record ALL customer calls (sales, onboarding, support, upsell, milestone)
- Clip "before" from sales calls, "during" from support, "after" from milestone/award calls
- Create labeling system for easy retrieval
- Local businesses: capture "upon delivery" reactions (ribbon cutting moments)

**Social Media Scrape:**
- Create hashtags and tags for community
- Set regular reminders to grab tagged media (especially stories that disappear)
- Separate best performers into dedicated folder
- Use best-performing organic content as ad creative

**In-Person Events (3 types):**
1. Stage Ads -- selfies/video with celebrating crowd behind you
2. Professional Testimonials:
   - Reserve separate room + hire extra camera team
   - Pre-select customers, print hit list on clipboard
   - Use 6-point testimonial script
   - Hook is most important part -- have them say multiple versions
3. Man-in-the-Street:
   - Quick soundbites during breaks
   - 10 core questions, 10+ responses each
   - Turn into fast-hitting highlight reels

**Communication & Communities Scrape:**
- Support/Account Rep Chats: screenshot wins, search key terms (sales, $, profit, leads, close)
- Community Wins: "wins of the week" posts, screenshot best, screen-record scrolling through
- Reviews Scrape: sort by newest on all review sites, screenshot good ones

**Bonuses & Awards:**
- Bonus Unlocks: create valuable giveaway, offer free if they make testimonial video
- Award Testimonials (4 forms of proof per person):
  1. Post with stats in group
  2. Video emailed to proof@
  3. Unboxing video
  4. In-person/on-stage presentation

**Competitions (1-2x per year):**
- Raw iPhone testimonial competition
- Winner = ad with most conversions over 30 days
- Prize: 6 months free service
- Announce, countdown, collect, run as ads, measure, announce winner

**Implementation Priority Order:**
1. Competition (big influx)
2. Chat/messaging scrape (already have it)
3. Community scrape
4. Reviews scrape
5. Social media scrape
6. Bonus unlocks
7. Award unlocks
8. Events
9. Lifecycle (most editing, highest quality)

## 8. Price Raise Playbook

**Subtitle:** How to Raise Prices Without Losing Customers

### Core Frameworks

**The RAISE Letter Framework:**
- **R** -- Remind them of value provided
- **A** -- Address the price change directly
- **I** -- Invest in their future (show how extra money benefits them)
- **S** -- Soften with loyalty reward (expiring discount)
- **E** -- Explain away concerns (PS statement)

**Virtuous Price Cycle (raising prices):**
- Client emotional investment INCREASES
- Perceived value INCREASES
- Results INCREASE
- Demandingness DECREASES
- More money to spend on customers = higher service

**Vicious Price Cycle (lowering prices):**
- Client emotional investment DECREASES
- Perceived value DECREASES
- Results DECREASE
- Demandingness INCREASES
- Less money = worse service

**Pricing & Conversion/Churn Relationship:**
| Price | Conv Rate | Sales | Churn | LTV | Total | Difference |
|-------|-----------|-------|-------|-----|-------|------------|
| $10 | 5% | 5 | 10% | $100 | $500 | -- |
| $20 | 4% | 4 | 10% | $200 | $800 | +60% |
| $100 | 2% | 2 | 33% | $300 | $600 | +20% |

Best price = $20 (test more between $20-$100)

### Key Principles

- "The single most important decision in evaluating a business is pricing power." -- Warren Buffett
- "There's no strategic advantage to being the second lowest price. But there is for being the highest." -- Dan Kennedy
- Doubling price with 20% fewer deals = massive profit increase
- If 10% profit business raises prices 20% and keeps sales same = TRIPLE profit
- Most business owners copy other broke business owners' pricing
- Richest customers are often the easiest, most grateful customers
- "People want your stuff for less. Doesn't mean they won't buy for more."

### Tactical Actions (Rules for Raising Prices)

1. Don't grandfather existing customers. Don't do lifetime deals.
2. Never sell one-time price for lifetime access (unless zero fulfillment cost)
3. It's OK to be insecure early -- but raise prices as things improve
4. Raise prices at least once per year (Warren Buffett raised See's Candies ~10%/year for 50+ years)
5. Test on new customers BEFORE rolling out to entire base
6. Meet with people individually if raising prices more than 50%
7. Pair price raise within 90 days of a launch/new product
8. Do the math: know what % of customers you can lose and still profit

**The RAISE Letter (detailed):**

**Section R -- Remind:** List specific value delivered. Personalize: # calls attended, revenue generated, features used.

**Section A -- Address:** One sentence. Rip off bandaid. "For us to continue to invest in [PRODUCT] for you, we need to increase our prices."

**Section I -- Invest:** Frame price increase as investment in their future. Categories: hiring better people, training, equipment, technology, facility upgrades, higher service. Pair each with: more good stuff (faster, easier, risk-free) or less bad stuff.

**Section S -- Soften:** "As of today we're raising prices on new customers, but since you've been loyal, we're keeping you on existing plan for 3-6 months. Giving you $XXX credit." Vanishing discount > price raise psychologically.

**Section E -- Explain:** PS statement: "If this will materially affect your ability to buy groceries / your business, let me know and we'll work something out."

**Three types of responses:**
1. See the value -- comply (majority)
2. Actually affected -- extend discount another 6 months
3. Were gonna cancel anyway -- "pulled forward" churn, returns to baseline by month 3

**For new customers:** Raise immediately. Sales team script: "That's actually good news -- we keep making the value better. 96% of customers agreed. This is the cheapest it'll ever be."

**Stair-step option:** Drop discount in stages ($200/mo off for 4 months, then $100, then $50). Easier to handle disappearing discounts than raised prices.

### Price Raise Checklist
- Decide on price increase
- Test with new customers first
- Segment old customers
- Write 1-5 bullets of current value (personalized)
- Tell them the price raise
- Write 3 bullets of investments you'll make
- Explain how investments benefit them
- Give expiring loyalty discount
- Tell them you respond personally
- Sign in ink if possible
- PS statement for hardship cases
- Individual calls if 50%+ raise
- Stair-step discount if needed

## 9. Retention Playbook (Churn Checklist)

**Subtitle:** How to Get Customers to Keep Buying

### Core Frameworks

**The 5 Horsemen of Retention (from gym data):**
1. Track attendance (3x/week = stay; 2x/week = churn risk; reach out immediately when drops)
2. Reach out 2x/week (praise progress, solve small problems)
3. Handwritten cards (sign up, 3/6/12 month milestones, holidays, birthdays)
4. Member events (every 21/42/63 days, handwritten invites)
5. Exit interviews (save ~half of phone/email cancellations)

**Results of implementing 5 Horsemen:**
- Month 1: churn UP 50% ("shaking the tree")
- Month 2: churn DOWN 50%
- Month 3: churn DOWN another 50%
- Net: 10% to 3% churn = 3.3x LTV increase

**Problem-Solving Framework (from DoD consulting):**
1. Ask experts about the problem
2. Get all their answers
3. Find common threads (common factors analysis)

**Inversion Technique:**
"What would make 100% of my customers leave?" then do the exact opposite.
- Ignore them -> Talk with them
- Break promises -> Keep promises
- Miscommunicate -> Communicate clearly
- Treat poorly -> Treat like royalty
- Set unrealistic expectations -> Set realistic ones
- Hide progress -> Give status updates
- Keep away from happy customers -> Connect them
- Make stuff hard to use -> Make it easy

**Customer Journey (4 milestones):**
1. Activate
2. Testimonial
3. Refer
4. Ascend (buy more)

### Key Principles

- "You're doing sales because you failed at marketing. You're doing marketing because you failed at product." -- Naval
- Reducing churn from 10% to 3% = 3.3x LTV
- 5x-25x more expensive to acquire new customer vs retain existing (Harvard)
- 5% increase in retention = 25-95% increase in profits (Harvard Business Review)
- If referrals > churn, you never stop growing
- "Think value per second, not seconds of value. Less but better > more and decent."
- Overwhelm is #1 reason for churn
- "It's easy to quit a membership, it's hard to leave a relationship."
- "They come for the bikini. They stay for the community."

**Price-Value-Churn Relationship:**
- Higher price = higher churn (generally)
- But not always proportional -- test to find optimal
- Across Skool: higher price = higher churn
- Match business model with pricing model

### Tactical Actions (9-Step Churn Checklist)

**#1 Figure Out Activation Points:**
- Find churned customers who stayed 3+ months
- Order by most money spent, take top 20%
- Learn everything about them (demographics, behavior, usage)
- Common factors = activation point candidates
- Update messaging to attract these customers
- Update onboarding to drive to activation point
- Retest every 6-12 months
- Example: Gym Launch found top 20% customers worth $42K vs competitor's $5K = 70x more profit

**#2 Onboard Customers:**
- Teach them how to hit activation points
- Custom > generic. Personal > group. Live > recorded. Carrots > sticks. Some > none.
- Outline how to get value -> drive to activation point
- Resell the value of purchase
- Tell them how to unlock more value over time
- Establish long-term communication cadence
- Optional: sell onboarding (e.g., 6 personal training sessions before group classes)
- Example: portfolio company went from group to 1-on-1 onboarding = 25% boost in ascensions, scaled from $2M/month to $2M/week

**#3 Incentivize Customer Activation:**
- Unlockables: courses, 1-on-1 calls, event tickets, higher tier access, badges, free lifetime access at level 8
- Place incentives just past major churn points (if most leave month 3, put something cool at month 4)
- Monitor "usage churn" -- still subscribed but stopped using = leading indicator

**#4 Community Linking:**
- Connect members to each other, not just to you
- Group events (1-on-6 onboarding intentionally)
- Manually connect people who would benefit from knowing each other
- Start community podcast, interview members
- Elevate micro-celebrities within community

**#5 Fire or Correct Bad Customers:**
- Delete bad posts, explain why, show what good looks like
- 3 strikes policy
- Pin best 1-2 new posts daily
- Create topic categories: wins, fun, discovered, meetups

**#6 Add Annual Pricing:**
- Annual option: "buy 10 months get 2 free" (16% off)
- If avg stay < 12 months, price annual ABOVE avg LTV
- Alternative: "big head, long tail" -- big upfront (education/setup) + small monthly recurring
- Example: $6,800 upfront + $199/mo -> LTV from $6,800 to $12,800
- Founder rates: 50% discount for business owners (incentivizes buy AND stay)

**#7 Exit Interviews / Cancellation Calls:**
- Set expectation during onboarding
- Save ~half of cancellations
- Save with a redo: "Let me give it another shot"
- Save with an upsell: "You should have been in the higher program, that's on me"
- Phone > email/text for saving
- Resell why staying helps their goals
- Tell them what they lose (custom URLs, posts, access, founder pricing, data)
- For high-volume/low-price: cancellation VIDEO that resells and shows what they lose

**#8 Survey Twice Per Year:**
- "If I removed everything but one, what would you keep?"
- "If I kept everything but one, which would you least miss?"
- Identify core 2-3 things. Do more of what they like. Cut what they don't.
- Reach out 1-on-1 every 2-3 weeks using ACA framework: Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask
- Counterintuitive: wealthier customers want LESS handholding

**#9 Customer Journey (4 Milestones):**
1. Activate (first)
2. Testimonial
3. Refer
4. Ascend (buy more)
- Incentivize each milestone with unlockables
- "Customers get an itch to buy more. They'll buy from you or the guy down the street."

### Retention Checklist
- Find activation points (top 20% customers, common factors)
- Onboard customers (1-on-1 if expensive, drive to activation)
- Use unlockables (courses, calls, events, badges, time-based unlocks)
- Community linking (events, manual connections, podcast, micro-celebrities)
- Fire/correct bad customers (3 strikes, pin good posts)
- Add annual pricing (buy 10 get 2 free, big-head-long-tail, founder rates)
- Cancellation calls/videos (resell, show what they lose)
- Survey 2x/year + regular 1-on-1 check-ins (ACA framework)
- Customer journey (activate, testimonial, refer, ascend)

## Cross-Playbook Formulas & Recurring Themes

### Universal Formulas

| Formula | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| LTV = Gross Profit / Churn | Lifetime value for recurring businesses |
| LTV = Gross Profit x Avg Transactions | Lifetime value for transactional businesses |
| 10x the 10% | 10% of customers at 10x price = double revenue |
| Sales Conv Rate x LTGP | Optimal price maximizes this product |
| Conversion Rate x Churn Rate | Both suffer as price rises, but not proportionally |
| Referrals > Churn | Compounding growth formula |
| 50 Hooks x 3-5 Meats x 1-3 CTAs | Ad assembly line output (150-750 ads/week) |

### Recurring Principles Across All Playbooks

1. **Test, test, test.** Pricing, hooks, ads, offers -- let data decide, not intuition.
2. **Volume negates luck.** More ads, more reach-outs, more offers, more tests.
3. **The boring work wins.** Execution > strategy. Do the tedious stuff.
4. **Existing customers are gold.** Cheaper to retain than acquire. Easier to sell.
5. **Make more, way more.** 5 ads/month to 500. 1 reach-out to 7+.
6. **Clear > clever.** In CTAs, pricing, communication.
7. **Show, don't tell.** Demonstrate value, proof, what happens next.
8. **Fortune favors the bold.** Raise prices, make premium offers, go bigger.
9. **Compounding beats everything.** Brand, retention, customer base, proof.
10. **There are no silver bullets, only golden BBs.** Many small things compound into massive results.
