## Core Frameworks

### 1. The Core Four (Advertising Methods)

The ONLY four ways any person can let other people know about anything. Two audience types (warm/cold) crossed with two communication types (1-to-1/1-to-many):

| Method | Audience | Communication | Description |
|--------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Warm Outreach** | Warm (people who know you) | 1-to-1 (private) | Calls, texts, DMs, emails to contacts |
| **Post Free Content** | Warm (people who know you) | 1-to-many (public) | Social media posts, videos, podcasts, articles |
| **Cold Outreach** | Cold (strangers) | 1-to-1 (private) | Cold calls, cold emails, cold DMs, door knocking |
| **Run Paid Ads** | Cold (strangers) | 1-to-many (public) | Facebook ads, Google ads, billboards, radio |

Key insight: If you aren't getting enough leads, you're not doing the core four with enough skill or enough volume.

### 2. The Four Lead Getters

People who do the core four ON YOUR BEHALF. Higher leverage than doing it yourself because there are more of them than there are of you.

| Lead Getter | Description | Key Strength |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Customer Referrals** | Customers who tell others about you | Biggest potential for low-cost exponential growth |
| **Employees** | People in your business trained to get leads | Direct influence, run business on your behalf |
| **Agencies** | Service businesses that get leads for you | Teach skills you keep forever and transfer to team |
| **Affiliates** | Independent businesses that tell their audience about your stuff | Once going, operate entirely on their own |

### 3. The Content Unit (Three Components)

All audience-growing content does one thing -- rewards the people consuming it. Three components:

1. **Hook** -- Get them to notice your content (measured by % who start consuming)
2. **Retain** -- Get them to keep consuming it (driven by curiosity: lists, steps, stories)
3. **Reward** -- Satisfy the reason they consumed it (value per second)

Short form = fewer hook-retain-reward cycles. Long form = more cycles chained together. "There is no such thing as too long, only too boring."

### 4. What-Who-When Framework (Ad Copy)

Used to create advertising angles that show WHY prospects should be interested:

**WHAT (8 Key Elements):**
1. Dream Outcome -- best possible results
2. Nightmare (opposite) -- worst hassles of going without
3. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement -- lowering perceived risk
4. Risk (opposite) -- how risky it is NOT to act
5. Time Delay -- how slow their current trajectory is
6. Speed (opposite) -- how much faster they get results
7. Effort & Sacrifice -- work/skill needed without your solution
8. Ease (opposite) -- how they avoid stuff they hate

**WHO (Status Perspectives):**
- The prospect themselves
- Spouse, Kids, Parents, Extended Family
- Colleagues, Bosses, Friends, Rivals, Competitors
- Each perspective reveals different status gains/losses

**WHEN (Timeline):**
- Past -- consequences of past decisions
- Present -- current situation
- Future -- what decisions lead to

Combining all three dimensions creates virtually unlimited advertising angles.

### 5. More Better New (Scaling Framework)

How to get even more leads from the core four:

1. **More** -- Do more of what's currently working. Double inputs = more leads. Apply the Rule of 100.
2. **Better** -- Test one thing per week per platform. Find the constraint (biggest drop-off point). Smallest improvement at constraint = biggest result increase.
3. **New** -- Only after exhausting more/better. Order: New placements > New platforms > New core four activity.

Key: "What's stopping them from doing 10x what they're currently doing?"

### 6. The Value Equation (from $100M Offers, referenced throughout)

Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

Goal: Maximize top two, minimize bottom two.

### 7. Problem-Solution Cycle (Lead Magnet Design)

Every problem has a solution. Every solution reveals more problems. Pick a narrow problem, solve it (lead magnet), the new problem revealed is solved by your core offer (paid).

### 8. Give:Ask Ratio

- Television: ~3.5:1 (47 min content to 13 min ads)
- Facebook: ~4:1 (4 posts per 1 ad)
- Growing platforms: dramatically over-give, under-ask
- Hormozi's recommendation: "Give until they ask" -- give in public, ask in private

### 9. ACA Framework (Warm Outreach Conversations)

- **A**cknowledge -- Restate what they said (shows active listening)
- **C**ompliment -- Tie to a positive character trait
- **A**sk -- Ask another question, leading toward your offer

### 10. Whisper-Tease-Shout (Launch Method)

For activating affiliates or launching any product:

- **Whisper** (6+ weeks out): Create curiosity. Keep product mysterious. Show behind-the-scenes. Short hints. Every 4-6 weeks, then every 2-3 weeks.
- **Tease** (30 days out): Reveal product. Make launch date public. Show elements of value. Use What-Who-When. Once/week, then twice/week.
- **Shout** (3 days out): Specific CTAs. Bonuses, scarcity, urgency, guarantees. Twice/day, then every few hours, then every 30 minutes on launch day.

### 11. The Leverage Ladder (Four Scenarios)

| Scenario | Work | Leads | Leverage |
|----------|------|-------|----------|
| You do core four alone | HIGH | LOW | LOW |
| You get one lead getter | LOW | LOW | HIGH |
| You get many lead getters | HIGH | HIGH | HIGHER |
| You get a lead getter who gets lead getters | LOW | HIGH | HIGHEST |

### 12. The Seven Levels of Advertisers (Roadmap to $100M)

1. **Level 1**: Friends know about your stuff. One offer, one avatar, one platform. Primary: Warm outreach.
2. **Level 2**: Consistently let everyone know. Know exact inputs for engaged leads. Maximize personal capacity. Primary: Warm outreach + posting content.
3. **Level 3**: Hire employees to do more advertising. Primary: Hire people to advertise profitably.
4. **Level 4**: Product good enough for consistent referrals (25%+). Double down on referrals + scale team. Primary: Product improvement + bigger team.
5. **Level 5**: Advertise in more places, more ways, more people. Multiple platforms, multiple methods. Primary: At least two methods on multiple platforms.
6. **Level 6**: Hire killer executives. Departments run without you. Primary: Battle-hardened executives for new channels.
7. **Level 7**: "I'll come back and edit this chapter once I cross a billion."

## Key Principles

### On Leads
- A **lead** is a person you can contact. That's all.
- An **engaged lead** is a person who shows interest in the stuff you sell. This is the true output of advertising.
- All else being equal, when you double your leads, you double your business.
- You get more customers by getting: More leads, Better leads, Cheaper leads, Reliably (from lots of places).

### On Advertising
- Advertising is the process of making known. It lets strangers know about the stuff you sell.
- Advertising lets you have a terrible product and still make money. Lets you be terrible at sales and still make money. Gives you endless chances to get it right.
- "The right action in the wrong amount still fails."
- "Most people dramatically underestimate the volume it takes to make advertising work."
- All advertising works. The only difference is how well it works (efficiency).
- Advertising always works, it's only a matter of efficiency.

### On Volume
- "Quantity has a quality all of its own." -- Napoleon
- 100 reach outs per day, not 100 over time.
- 300 flyers = 1/1500th the effort required. Test with 5000, then 5000/day for a month.
- "I would never let effort be the reason anything didn't work for me."
- "Do more than they do, and you will have more than they have."

### On Content
- "The content you create isn't the compounding asset -- the audience is."
- "Building an audience is the most valuable thing I've ever done."
- Free content makes ALL other advertising more effective. (78% of Gym Launch clients consumed at least TWO long-form pieces before booking a call.)
- "People don't have shorter attention spans, they have higher standards."
- Switch from "How to" to "How I" -- talk about what you've done, not what others should do.
- "We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught." (1 in 5 people who saw daily book posts didn't know he had a book.)
- Content creates tools for salespeople. Create a master list of "greatest hits."
- Free content retains paying customers.

### On Lead Magnets
- A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem.
- Three types: (1) Reveal Problems, (2) Samples and Trials, (3) One Step of a Multi-Step Process
- Four delivery methods: Software, Information, Services, Physical Products
- 3 types x 4 delivery methods = up to 12 lead magnets for a single narrow problem
- "Give away the secrets, sell the implementation."
- "Make your lead magnets as good as your paid stuff. Your reputation depends on it."
- 99% of people aren't gonna buy, but they will create (or destroy) your reputation based on the value of your free stuff.

### On Offers & Value
- "Make your offer so good people feel stupid saying no."
- Grand Slam Offers work for free stuff as much or better than paid stuff.
- "People buy stuff based on how much value they think they'll get AFTER they buy it."
- Hidden costs (time, effort, sacrifice) are often more expensive than the price.
- If you struggle to give stuff away for free, either people don't want it, don't believe you, or the hidden costs are too high.

### On Testing
- Test one thing per week per platform.
- Focus testing on the constraint (biggest drop-off point).
- 5% improvement at a constraint with 5% throughput = 100% increase in leads vs 10-16% at other steps.
- If you can't beat current version in four tries (one month), move to next constraint.
- Log all test results so you start improvements later, not at square one.
- Budget for testing: 2x the cash collected from a customer in 30 days.

### On Referrals
- Price is what you charge. Value is what they get. The difference = goodwill.
- Goodwill creates word of mouth. Word of mouth = referrals.
- Nothing scales like word of mouth (exponential, not linear).
- Referral Growth Equation: Referrals (in) - Churned customers (out). If referrals > churn, you grow without other advertising.
- Two reasons businesses don't get referrals: (1) Product isn't good enough, (2) They don't ask.
- "If your product were exceptional, people would already know about it."
- "Why are my customers too embarrassed to tell everyone they know about my product?"

### On Employees
- "If you want it done right, get someone to spend all their time doing it."
- "If I can do it, someone else can do it better."
- "Everyone is replaceable, especially me."
- You get rich from what you make. You become wealthy from what you own.
- A business that runs without you is worth $10M+ more than one that requires you.

### On Agencies
- Use agencies for learning new methods and new platforms, not as a permanent crutch.
- "I want to do what you do in my business, but I don't know how. I'd like to work with 6 months so I can learn."
- Hire one "good enough" agency to learn the ropes, then a more elite agency to maximize.
- Compare your team's results to agency's until you beat them, then cancel.
- Good agencies: referral-based, waiting list, clear process, realistic expectations, expensive.

### On Mindset
- "Yeses give me opportunity. Nos give me feedback. Either way, I win."
- "You either win or you learn."
- "You cannot lose if you do not quit."
- "Success comes down to doing the obvious thing for an uncommonly long period of time without convincing yourself you're smarter than you are." -- Neil Strauss
- "If someone is making more money than you, they are better at the game of business in some way. Take it as good news."
- "People try shortcuts for a decade until they realize they should have picked a strategy and stuck with it for a decade."
- "I am compensated tomorrow for the value I provide today."

## Formulas & Equations

### LTGP:CAC Ratio
- **LTGP** (Lifetime Gross Profit) = Total revenue from customer - Total cost to deliver
- **CAC** (Cost to Acquire Customer) = Total advertising spend / Number of customers acquired
- **LTGP:CAC Ratio** = LTGP / CAC
- Target: At minimum 3:1. Below 3:1 = struggling to scale. Above 3:1 = scale aggressively.
- Example: $100 LTGP / $30 CAC = 3.3:1

### Client Financed Acquisition
- Cash collected in first 30 days >= CAC + Fulfillment cost
- Break even in 30 days = free customers = unlimited scaling (use credit card for 30-day float)
- Example: $15/mo membership, $5 cost = $10 profit. CAC = $30. Add $100 upsell (1 in 5 buy) = $20 avg. $10 + $20 = $30 in 30 days = break even.
- "This is how I've scaled every company I've started for the past seven years past $1M/mo in the first twelve months -- without outside funding."

### Warm Outreach Math
- 100 warm reach outs -> ~20 replies (20%) -> ~4 take free offer (20%) -> ~1 customer (25%)
- 1 customer per 100 reach outs
- 500 reach outs/week x $400 product = 5 customers/week = $2000/week = $104,000/year

### Cold Outreach Math (Phone)
- 100 cold calls/day -> 20% pick up = 20 -> 25% want lead magnet = 4 engaged leads
- 4 engaged leads in ~4 hours = 1 engaged lead per hour

### Cold Outreach Math (Email)
- 100 personalized emails -> 30% open -> 10% reply = 3 engaged leads (3% engagement rate)
- Target: 3% of list turning into engaged leads

### Referral Impact on LTGP:CAC
- Without referrals: 4:1 LTGP:CAC (25% of LTGP to acquire)
- With every customer bringing 2 referrals: 12:1 LTGP:CAC (8.3% of LTGP to acquire)

### Paid Ads Budget
- Reverse-engineer from sales goals: (Number of customers wanted) x (CAC) x 1.2 (20% pad) = Monthly budget
- Example: 100 customers x $100 CAC x 1.2 = $12,000/month = $400/day

### Employee ROI
- Total Payroll / Total Engaged Leads = Cost per engaged lead
- Example: $100,000 payroll / 1000 leads = $100 per engaged lead
- If 1 in 10 become customers: $100 x 10 = $1000 CAC
- If LTGP = $4000: 4:1 ratio

### Affiliate LTGP:CAC
- Example: $4000 CAC to acquire affiliate. Affiliate sells $10K/mo for 12 months = $120K total sales. 75% margins = $90K gross profit. 40% payout = $36K to affiliate. $120K - $30K costs - $36K payouts = $54K leftover. $54K / $4K = 13.5:1

### Affiliate Tiered Payout
- Tier 1: 25% of max CAC (anyone who signs up)
- Tier 2: 50% of max CAC (once activated)
- Tier 3: 100% of max CAC (sustained performance)
- Blended payout < max CAC = extra profit margin

## Step-by-Step Processes

### 7 Steps to Creating an Effective Lead Magnet
1. Figure out the problem you want to solve and who to solve it for (narrow + meaningful)
2. Figure out how to solve it (Reveal Problem, Sample/Trial, or One Step of Multi-Step)
3. Figure out how to deliver it (Software, Information, Services, Physical Products)
4. Test what to name it (headlines, images, subheadlines -- in that priority order)
5. Make it easy to consume (multiple formats: video, text, audio, images)
6. Make it darn good ("Give away the secrets, sell the implementation")
7. Make it easy for them to tell you they want more (CTA with scarcity, urgency, reasons)

### 10 Steps for Warm Outreach
1. Get your list (phone contacts, email, social media -- everyone you know)
2. Pick a platform (the one with the most contacts)
3. Personalize your message (use something you know about them)
4. Reach out to 100 people per day (up to 3 times each, once/day until they respond)
5. Warm them up (ACA Framework: Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask)
6. Invite their friends ("Do you know anyone who is [struggle] looking to [dream outcome]?")
7. Make them the easiest offer in the world (FREE for first 5 case studies)
8. Start back at the top (cycle through all platforms)
9. Start charging (80% off for next 5, then 60%, then 40%, raise 20% every 5)
10. Keep your list warm (regular value + Dean Jackson's 9-word email: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?")

### 3 Steps for Cold Outreach
1. Build a list (Software scraping > Brokers > Manual/Elbow grease)
2. Personalize + Big Fast Value (1-3 personal details + offer something people actually pay for, free)
3. Volume (Automate delivery, automate distribution, follow up more times in more ways, restart list every 3-6 months)

### 4 Steps for Paid Ads
1. Pick a platform (you've used it, can target your audience, know the format, have minimum budget)
2. Target the right audience (lookalike audiences from customer lists + demographic/interest filters)
3. Create the ad (Callout + Value Elements + CTA)
4. Get permission to contact them (landing page for contact info)

### 3 Phases of Scaling Paid Ads
1. **Track Money** -- Set up tracking before spending a dollar
2. **Lose Money** -- Budget 2x 30-day cash collected per customer to test. 9/10 ads lose. Find the 1 winner and 100x down.
3. **Print Money** -- Making more than spending. Reverse budget from sales goals. Spend as much as you can.

### 6 Ways to Get More Referrals (By Giving More Value)
1. Sell Better Customers (target customers who get the most value)
2. Set Better Expectations (slowly lower promises, keep quality, overdeliver)
3. Get More People Better Results (survey best customers, find common actions, force those actions for everyone)
4. Make Faster Wins (deliver at shorter intervals, frequent updates, force wins in first 48 hours, BAMFAM: Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting)
5. Keep Making Your Stuff Better (use customer service data/surveys to find top problem, fix it, measure, repeat monthly)
6. Tell Them What To Buy Next (never stop selling, more products = more value = more goodwill)

### 7 Ways to Ask for Referrals
1. One-sided referral benefit (pay CAC to referrer OR give discount to friend)
2. Two-sided referral benefits (split CAC between referrer and friend -- Dropbox/PayPal model)
3. Ask right when they buy (on contract/checkout: "Who else would you want to do this with?")
4. Referrals as negotiation chip ("I can't do less than $500, but if you introduce 3 friends, I'll cut the initiation fee")
5. Referral events (1-4 week "Bring-a-friend" / "Spouse Challenge" / "Accountability Buddy" promotions)
6. Ongoing referral programs (talk about benefits of doing things with others all the time -- 33% boost in signups)
7. Unlockable referral bonuses (VIP bonuses, courses, tokens, status for referring + leaving testimonials)

### 3Ds Employee Training Model
1. **Document** -- Make a checklist of exact steps as you do the job. Record yourself. Test: can you do A+ job only following directions?
2. **Demonstrate** -- Walk them through the checklist step by step. Adjust checklist when they slow down.
3. **Duplicate** -- They do it in front of you following the checklist. Fix checklist until outcome is right. Practice until they get it right.

### How to Use Agencies (Hormozi Method)
1. Open with: "I want to learn how you do it in 6 months. I'll pay extra for you to explain your decisions."
2. Hire one "good enough" agency to learn ropes
3. Hire a more elite agency (up to 4x price) for in-depth maximization
4. Compare your team's results to agency's
5. When your team beats them regularly, drop to consulting-only arrangement
6. Cut loose when you're teaching them instead of learning

### 6 Steps to Build an Affiliate Army
1. Find ideal affiliates (Who's got my leads? What do my best customers buy, where do they go, what do they do?)
2. Make them an offer (new way to make money: dream outcome, perceived likelihood, time delay, effort/sacrifice)
3. Qualify them (make them invest: become a customer + become a certified expert. Charge 10-20% of average affiliate's first-year earnings)
4. Figure out what to pay them (3-tier payout: 25% CAC for sign-up, 50% for activation, 100% for sustained performance)
5. Get them advertising -- Launch (whisper-tease-shout method)
6. Keep them advertising -- Integrate (give away your lead magnet, sell your lead magnet, or sell your core offer directly)

### One-Page Advertising Plan
1. Pick the type of engaged lead to get: Customers, Affiliates, Employees, or Agencies
2. Pick Rule of 100 or Open to Goal. Commit to daily advertising actions.
3. Fill out the Advertising Checklist for that daily action.
4. Do this daily action until you can afford paying someone else to do it.
5. Go back to step 1. Make employees your new target lead type. Repeat until you have help. Then scale again.

## Action Items / Tactical Advice

### Immediate Actions (Do Today)
1. Count all your contacts across phone, email, and social media. That's your first lead list.
2. Pick the platform with the most contacts and start reaching out to 100/day.
3. Use the warm outreach script: "Do you know anyone who is [struggle] looking to [dream outcome]?"
4. Make your first 5 clients free to get reps, feedback, and testimonials.
5. Start posting content on one platform. Make your first business post today.
6. Search "HOW TO PLACE A [PLATFORM] AD" and spend $100 on your first ad.

### Pricing Ladder
- Start free for first 5 clients
- 80% off for next 5
- 60% off for next 5
- 40% off for next 5
- Continue raising 20% every 5 until you find your sweet spot

### Content Scaling Strategy
- Either depth-then-width (master one platform, then add next) or width-then-depth (get on all platforms early, then maximize all at once)
- Depth-then-width: fewer resources needed, leverages compounding
- Width-then-depth: broader reach faster, more repurposing efficiency
- Best day to start posting content was the day you were born. Second best is today.

### Cold Outreach Tactical
- Get targeted lists via: (1) Software scraping tools, (2) List brokers, (3) Manual scraping from groups/communities
- Personalize with 1-3 pieces of info a friend might know
- Lead with email first (most don't respond), then use non-response as reason to follow up via another channel
- Contact each lead multiple times in multiple ways
- Wait 3-6 months then restart the same list (circumstances change)
- Expect 2-3 conversations before a higher ticket sale with strangers

### Paid Ads Tactical
- Callout types: Labels, Yes-Questions, If-Then Statements, Ridiculous Results
- Non-verbal callouts: Contrast (bright colors, attractive people, movement), Likeness (match your audience), Scene (show the problem visually)
- Local ads: "LOCAL AREA + TYPE OF PERSON" is one of the all-time best callouts
- "Quack like a duck" -- if you want to attract plumbers, dress like a plumber, talk like a plumber
- Landing pages: simple, match your ads, carry same look and language
- The simpler your landing page, the easier to test. Focus on words and image.

### Referral Tactical
- Gift card strategy: Give everyone a gift card for 1/3 the cost of their program. 7-14 day expiration. Customer gives to friend as a gift (higher status). Write friend's name on card before texting picture.
- Ask for referrals on the sales contract/checkout page
- Ask for spouse -- always offer household discount
- Ask every customer: "People who do this with someone else get 3x results. Who could you do this with?"

### Affiliate Tactical
- Find affiliates by asking: What do my best customers buy? Where do they go? What do they do? Who employs them?
- Make affiliates buy and use the product to keep status
- Charge 10-20% of average affiliate's first-year earnings for onboarding
- Do all the advertising work FOR affiliates (plug and play materials)
- Three integration strategies: (1) Give away your lead magnet free with their purchase, (2) Sell your lead magnet, (3) Sell your core offer
- Never cap payouts. Pay forever to keep affiliates motivated.

### Diagnosing Problems
- If CAC > 3x industry average: advertising or sales problem
- Question: "Do my engaged leads have the problem I solve and the money to spend?"
  - If NO: advertising problem (wrong leads)
  - If YES but not buying: sales problem
  - If YES and buying but not enough: advertising problem (need more volume)
- Don't confuse sales problems with advertising problems. Don't fire ad people for sales problems or vice versa.

### Personal Productivity (Hormozi's Stack)
1. Wake up early (4-5am) -- really means going to bed early
2. Get right to work -- no rituals, no routines, just coffee and work
3. No meetings until noon -- fully focused work time
4. Set daily goal for dedicated block, then handle fires/meetings after

### Key Ratios & Benchmarks
- LTGP:CAC minimum viable: 3:1
- Warm outreach engagement: ~20% reply rate, ~20% of replies accept offer
- Cold email engagement target: 3% of list to engaged leads
- Cold call pick-up rate: ~20%, then 25% want lead magnet
- Referral target: 25%+ of customers from referrals (Level 4)
- Give:Ask ratio minimum: 3:1 (like TV). Better: 10:1+. Best: give until they ask.
- Content consumption before buying: 78% consumed at least 2 long-form pieces (Gym Launch data)
- Testing ads: expect to lose on 9/10 ads. Win big on the 1 winner.
- Time to crack a new lead source: 3-6 months (even for experienced operators)
- Time to $100M: 5-10 years for business owners who know what to do
