## Core Frameworks

### The $100M Money Model
A Money Model is a **deliberate sequence of offers** -- what you offer, when you offer, and how you offer it to make as much money as you can as fast as you can. The goal: make enough money from ONE customer to get and service at least TWO more customers in less than 30 days.

### The Four Offer Types
Every Money Model combines four types of offers:

| Type | Purpose | When Used |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Attraction Offers** | Turn strangers into customers | First -- get customers and initial cash |
| **Upsell Offers** | Get people to spend more | Second -- maximize 30-day profits |
| **Downsell Offers** | Get people to say yes when they would have said no | Alongside upsells -- capture lost revenue |
| **Continuity Offers** | Keep people buying | Last -- stack recurring revenue |

### Three Stages of a Money Model
- **Stage I: Get Cash** -- Attraction Offers get more customers for less
- **Stage II: Get More Cash** -- Upsell and Downsell Offers make more money from them faster
- **Stage III: Get The Most Cash** -- Continuity Offers maximize their total money spent

### Money Model Evolution Pattern
1. Get customers reliably
2. Make sure they pay for themselves reliably
3. Make sure they pay for OTHER customers reliably
4. Maximize each customer's long-term value
5. Spend as many advertising dollars as possible to print money

## Key Principles

1. **A Money Model is a sequence of offers.** Different offers solve different problems. Making one offer works better than none. Making more offers works better than one. Combining offers in a sequence makes a Money Model.

2. **All businesses have Money Models.** Switch the poor person mantra "this won't work for my business" to the rich person mantra "how will I make this work for my business?"

3. **Your first offer doesn't always make the profit.** The thing you sell the most isn't always the thing you make the most profit on. You make it on the second, third, and fourth offers.

4. **Hard selling is for weak products.** If someone doesn't want something, that's OK. Make offers available when customers realize they have a problem.

5. **If a customer asks for their money back, give it back.** Don't take blood money. Focus on getting the next customer.

6. **Be transparent.** State the facts. If the facts aren't compelling, change reality to make them compelling. Don't lie -- a bad reputation sticks for life.

7. **We don't get customers to make a sale, we make sales to get customers.** Long-term thinking always wins.

8. **Simple scales. Fancy fails.** It's less about having 100 products to offer, and more about having 100 ways to offer your product.

9. **Every offer can be used on its own, at any time, in any order.** But when used in the right sequence and at the right time, they make a $100M Money Model.

10. **Any time "free" is mentioned, you can also use "discount" or "$1."** They exist on a continuum because they all discount a product to some level -- even 100%.

11. **Perfect one offer at a time.** Don't implement a whole Money Model at once. Pick one offer. Try it. Keep doing it until it works reliably. Then make it automatic. Then go to the next stage.

12. **Patience is still the fastest way to get to your goal.** Measure in quarters, not weeks. You either build it right or you build it again.

13. **Price new offers low enough to get lots of yeses.** Use customer feedback to improve. Then start raising the price until you stop making more money.

## Step-by-Step Processes

### Section II: Attraction Offers (5 Types)

#### 1. Win Your Money Back
**Structure:** Pay now with a chance to get money back later.
- Customer pays. If they hit X goal within Y time following Z rules, they get money back (cash or store credit).
- Three accountability types: Results-based, Actions-based, or Both.
- **Criteria must be:** Easy to track, gets customers results, advertises the business.
- **Store credit application:** Spread $600 credit over 12 months = $50/mo discount (keeps them engaged).
- **Key stat:** ~10% of customers ask for money back. This offer has generated over $1 billion in sales industry-wide.
- **Make everyone a winner:** Halfway through, offer credit toward next program whether they hit goal or not.
- **Only use if refund rate is below 5%.** Otherwise fix your product first.

#### 2. Giveaways
**Structure:** Enter for a chance to win a big prize. One person wins. Everyone else gets a discounted offer.
- Pick a Grand Prize (the thing you want everyone to buy, assign monetary value as price anchor).
- Pick promotional offer (partial scholarship/gift card/voucher -- 10-30% off gross margins).
- Ask for contact info + eligibility criteria + qualifying actions.
- Put on 3-7 day deadline.
- Announce winner publicly, contact everyone else privately with promotional offer.
- **Double leads trick:** Give away TWO prizes -- if someone they refer wins, they win one too.
- **Rule of thumb:** Discount = 10-30% of gross margins. Compare value to price for massive perceived savings (10% discount = 64% cost-to-value difference).

#### 3. Decoy Offer
**Structure:** Advertise something free/discounted. Present both a decoy (basic) and premium option side-by-side.
- Strip decoy: fewer components, older models, no guarantees.
- Load premium: more features, benefits, bonuses, guarantees, personalization.
- **Key question to get permission:** "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"
- 80% take rate on premium when contrast is big enough.
- **Surprise benefit:** Give decoy-takers a few free premium features to build goodwill.
- **Four ways to advertise discounts:** Percentage Off, Absolute Amount, Free Portion, Total Package.

#### 4. Buy X Get Y Free
**Structure:** Buy something, get other stuff free. More free stuff = better.
- Reframe pricing: "Buy 1 Get 2 Free" = same as buying 3, but perceived as much more valuable.
- **Rule:** Give more free than paid. "Buy 2 Get 10 Free" beats "Buy 10 Get 2 Free."
- Free things CAN be different from paid things.
- Raise prices before giving stuff away to preserve profits.
- Make this offer to existing customers for fast cash (limit to 10% of customer base).
- **Service version:** "Buy 6 Months Get 12 Months Free" (most compelling -- most free stuff).

#### 5. Pay Less Now or Pay More Later
**Structure:** Pay full-price later OR pay discounted price + bonuses now.
- Pay Later: $0 now, billed $X after Y days. Cancel before billing if unsatisfied (conditional guarantee).
- Pay Now: 20-50% discount + bonuses.
- **Promise a clear yes/no result.** Simple, measurable.
- If >10% cancel: promised too much, conditions too low, or price too high.
- Works for recurring revenue: higher ongoing rate in 30 days, or pay less today and keep lower rate for life.

### Section III: Upsell Offers (4 Types)

#### 1. The Classic Upsell
**Structure:** "You can't have X without Y!" Offer solution to next problem the moment they become aware of it.
- Core offer solves one problem and CREATES another. Upsell solves that next problem.
- **Tactics:**
  - "You don't want anything else, do you?" (no = yes)
  - Surprise and Delight: save bonuses, give all four even if they say yes early
  - Sell more during "hyper buying cycles" (weddings, new hobbies, babies, moves)
  - Use free bonuses to CREATE problems that upsells solve
  - BAMFAM: Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting
  - Bundle upsells and name them by customer type/result
  - Upsell guarantees, warranties, insurance (add 5-50% onto price)

#### 2. Menu Upsell
Combines four tactics:
1. **Unselling:** Cross out what they DON'T need (builds trust, highlights what they DO need)
2. **Prescription Upselling:** Tell them exactly what they need and HOW to use it (personalized instructions)
3. **A/B Upselling:** Ask preference between two options (either = sale). "Chocolate or vanilla?"
4. **Card On File:** "Wanna use the card on file?" (removes friction of taking card out)
- **Nudge:** "This is my favorite" or "a lot of people love this."
- If sold out, take payment and delay delivery.
- Employees love unselling -- let them "game the system."

#### 3. Anchor Upsell
**Structure:** Present super expensive thing first (5-10x). Get "The Gasp." Come to rescue with main offer.
1. Present the Anchor (really expensive thing)
2. Get "The Gasp" (customer freaks out)
3. Come to the rescue ("Do you care about what makes it premium?")
4. Present main offer (customer feels relieved)
5. Ask how they wanna pay
- **Key:** Don't treat anchor as fake -- actually sell it. Some customers WILL buy it.
- Primary features stay the same. Only secondary features differ.
- The bigger the gasp, the more they bought.

#### 4. Rollover Upsell
**Structure:** Credit some/all of customer's previous purchases toward your next offer.
- **Who:** Old customers, upset customers, competitors' upset customers, current customers.
- **What:** More of something, something better, something new/different. Always more expensive.
- **How:** Full or partial credit, up front or spread over time.
- **Pricing rule:** Next offer at least 4x higher than credit (25% max discount).
- **Winback campaign example:** Personalized videos for 200 past customers offering $4,000 credit. 20% take rate = ~$1,900,000 annual revenue from one day of recording.
- Add urgency: one-time only, take it now.
- Do Rollover Upsells BEFORE refunding.
- Scrape negative reviews for hot leads to rollover.

### Section IV: Downsell Offers (3 Types)

#### Rules of Downselling
- They said no to THIS offer, not ALL offers.
- Downsells are trades. Give something, get something.
- Personalize, don't pressure.
- Don't drop price just to get someone to buy (that's discounting, not downselling).
- Customers talk about price -- never charge different people different amounts for same thing.

#### 1. Payment Plan Downsell (7 Steps)
1. Present at full price, offer discount if they prepay (interest included as base price)
2. Offer 3rd-party financing, credit card, layaway
3. Half now, half later (aligned with paychecks)
4. Temperature check: "Scale of 1-10 how bad do you wanna do this?" (need 8+)
5. Split into three payments
6. Evenly spread payments over service duration
7. Offer Free Trial (Trial With Penalty -- next chapter)

**Seesaw Downsell shortcut:** "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" → Highlight prepay benefits → Adjust down payment until monthly rate works.

**Payment Plan Built-In Upsell:** Periodically offer the original prepay discount if they pay off balance.

#### 2. Trial With Penalty
**Structure:** Try for free IF you meet terms. If you don't meet terms, you pay.
- Terms = actions that make excellent customers (attendance, homework, check-ins).
- Always get credit card first, then explain fees.
- "If you do X, Y, Z, I'll let you start for free."
- Always sell staying and paying: "If this gets you the result, will you stay long-term?"
- Break fees into small amounts per missed item ($50 per miss) rather than one lump fee.
- **Upsell from trial outcomes:**
  - If they like it: offer longer term or higher value version
  - If they hate it: get angry at yourself, offer to make it up with better-fit service
  - If they didn't use it: reach out multiple times, waive fee if they do a meeting

#### 3. Feature Downsells
**Structure:** Take something away, lower the price, ask "how about now?"
- Lower quantity, lower quality, lower-price alternatives, or remove optional features entirely.
- **Key insight:** People see the value in what you removed AFTER they see the price difference.
- Remove high-value features first to get them to reconsider original offer.
- **Removing guarantees is a powerful Feature Downsell.** Tripled close rates in one case (25% to 75%).
- **Name feature combinations:** "The Whale Package," "Total Transformation," "The Minimum"
- Temperature check after two downsells (scale 1-10).
- Alternate between Payment Plan and Feature Downsells -- very difficult to refuse.
- "Deal?" or "Fair enough?" after each downsell.
- **Barter:** Discount in exchange for reviews, testimonials, referrals, social posts.
- **Feature downsell current customers** before they cancel -- second highest value customers.
- Done-For-You → Do-It-Yourself product downsell (chiropractor → home massage tools).

### Section V: Continuity Offers (3 Types)

#### 1. Continuity Bonus Offers
**Structure:** Give awesome thing (bonus) if they sign up today. Bonus value > first payment.
- Advertise the bonus, not the membership.
- Bonuses: more of same, complementary, upgrade.
- Keep bonuses related to core offer.
- Use stuff you already have (past content, onboarding you already do).
- Physical bonuses on digital products and vice versa.
- Stack bonuses: "on top of that...amazing thing 1, amazing thing 2, amazing thing 3."
- **Give customers titles** after 3, 6, 12+ months (silver, gold, diamond). Customers care more about title than any other bonus.
- Anchor bonuses first, then reveal how to get them free through membership.

#### 2. Continuity Discount Offers
**Structure:** Give free time if customer commits to buying over time.
- **Four ways to apply discount:**
  1. Up Front: free time first, paid time after (best for enforceable contracts)
  2. At The End: earn free time by making every payment on time
  3. Spread Over Time: $600 discount / 12 months = $50/mo off each month
  4. After first 1-2 payments: "first and last month" / activation fee before bonus
- **Lifetime Discount at churn point:** Lower rate if they stay past month X (where most cancel).
- **Gift cards:** Give discount as physical gift card. Customer can gift to friend = lead magnet. Many forget to use = full-price sign-up.
- **Cancellation policy:** Fee = discount they received. Simple to explain.
- Exit interviews save 1/3 of customers who agree to them.
- Waive cancellation fee if they do exit interview.

#### 3. Waived Fee Offers
**Structure:** Month-to-month with big setup fee, OR waive fee if they commit to a year.
- Fee = 3-5x monthly rate.
- If they cancel early, they pay the waived fee.
- Fee drops off after commitment fulfilled.
- Works best for 1+ year commitments and services that take long to work (SEO, investing, weight loss).
- Smaller fee = more up front cash. Larger fee = more commitments.
- **Cancellation fees for a cause:** "What cause do you hate? If you cancel, your fee goes to them."

### Section VI: Make Your Money Model (4 Steps)
1. Start with an Attraction Offer (may take up to a year to dial in)
2. Pick an Upsell Offer (solve the problems your Attraction Offer creates)
3. Pick a Downsell Offer (change how they pay or what they get)
4. Pick a Continuity Offer (one last sale + stack recurring cash)

## Action Items / Tactical Advice

### Immediate Revenue Boosters
1. **Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly.** 13 cycles vs 12 = 8.3% more revenue. 41% more profit on 20% margin business. Zero extra work.
2. **Add a 3% processing fee.** "It's $X plus a 3% processing fee." 30% more profit on a 10% margin business.
3. **Get two forms of payment.** Offer to waive processing fee for backup payment method. Prevents failed payments.
4. **Get ACH if you can.** Cheapest transaction method besides cash.
5. **Align payment schedules with paycheck schedules.** Fewer declines. Retry same day for ~1/3 recovery.
6. **Upsell guarantees, warranties, insurance instead of giving free.** Add 5-50% onto price.

### Sales Process Improvements
7. Always present premium/expensive option first.
8. Use "You don't want anything else, do you?" to get agreement through "no."
9. After two failed downsells, do temperature check (1-10 scale, need 8+).
10. End every appointment by scheduling the next one (BAMFAM).
11. Put product in customers' hands before they say yes (harder to give back than say no).
12. "Deal?" or "Fair enough?" after every downsell adjustment.

### Offer Design
13. Always have something to sell after the first offer.
14. Make Attraction Offer bonuses that CREATE upsell opportunities.
15. For feature downsells, remove highest-value features first (gets people to reconsider original).
16. Name cheapest package "The Minimum" (implies they have to get at least that).
17. Create affiliate relationships to fill Money Model gaps without operational headache.
18. Turn Attraction Offers into Continuity with automatic renewal at end of term.

### Customer Retention
19. Feature downsell current customers before they cancel (proactively lower unused features).
20. Use exit interviews: waive cancellation fee if they agree. Saves 1/3 of customers.
21. Offer lifetime discount at your most common churn month.
22. Give customers titles/status after milestones (3, 6, 12 months) -- they care more about titles than other bonuses.
23. Remind customers about upcoming bonuses to keep them excited about staying.

### Cash Flow
24. Offer bulk prepaid discounts: "buy 5 months get 1 free." Only 1/8 takers needed to raise 30-day profits 50%.
25. Make standalone offers 1.33-2.66x more expensive than continuity to push people toward recurring.
26. Give gift cards for discounts -- many forget to use them = full-price sign-up + potential referral.
