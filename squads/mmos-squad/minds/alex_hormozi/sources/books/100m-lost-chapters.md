## Core Frameworks

### Your First Avatar (Vista Method)
A 4-step process adapted from how Vista Equity Partners (a $50B+ PE fund) evaluates and grows companies:

1. **Survey your customers** -- Demographics, business stats (before/current), aspirations, buying process (trigger events, content consumed, referral source, time from first contact to purchase)
2. **Find your biggest spenders** -- Sort by customers who stay longest and pay most. Focus on top 20%. Ignore the rest.
3. **See what they have in common** -- Read through all answers, find the fewest qualifiers they all share (usually 3-5).
4. **Execute** two things:
   - a) **Speak your new avatar:** Be upfront about customer requirements in ALL advertising. Repel bad, attract good. Stop selling anyone who doesn't meet ideal requirements. Increase effort on channels these people come through.
   - b) **Re-engineer the sales process:** Reverse-engineer the buying journey of your best customers and make it happen on purpose.

### Pareto on Steroids
20% of customers bring 80% of revenue. If you replace the 80% with high spenders, you grow the business 5x. This insight drove the Value Acceleration Method (VAM) at Acquisition.com.

### Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA) -- Full Math
Three levers to make CFA work:
1. **Lower CAC** (Cost to Acquire a Customer)
2. **Increase LTGP** (Lifetime Gross Profit)
3. **Decrease PPD** (Payback Period)

**CFA Formula:**
> 30 Day Gross Profit > CAC (Level 2)
> 30 Day Gross Profit > 2x CAC (Level 3 -- Alex's minimum standard)

### Three Levels of CFA
| Level | Description | Cash Implication |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Level 1** | GP < CAC in first 30 days | Floating on savings/loans/credit. Sucks. |
| **Level 2** | GP = CAC in first 30 days | Credit limit = advertising budget. Can grow but capped. |
| **Level 3** | GP > 2x CAC in first 30 days | Can double business every month. Customers pay for themselves + next customer. |

### The Value Grid
An alternative to the "value ladder" -- a grid showing all offers, take rates, and revenue per client across the customer journey. Unlike a stair-step (linear), the grid reflects reality: customers skip offers, take non-adjacent ones, etc.

**Simple Grid Example:** 10 prospects → 8 take trial → 3 convert paid → $600 total → $75 30D Cash per prospect
**Stacked Grid Example:** Same 10 prospects with multiple offers → $1,763 30D Cash per prospect = **14-15x more revenue**

### Advanced Offer Stacking Process
**Sequence:** 1) Attract → 2) Up Front Cash → 3) Upsell/Downsell → 4) Continuity
Can be repeated: → 5) Continuity #2 → 6) Up Front Cash #2

### Four Steps to Picking the Right Offer
1. **Right Stage** -- Does the offer fit Attraction, Upsell/Downsell, or Continuity?
2. **Right Problem** -- Can you solve it with existing resources? Does it provide big value?
3. **Right Way** -- Solve based on customer's preference, not yours
4. **Right Time** -- Offer at the moment of greatest need, not when convenient for you

### Three Core Limiting Beliefs (For Presentations)
1. **Results must come easy** -- Product is easy to use, lives up to claims, universe is fair now that they have this
2. **I must get the best results possible** -- Past failures weren't their fault, this product prevents future failure
3. **Results must lead to attention and approval (status)** -- Product positions them above others, people will treat them the way they want

### The Performance Diamond (Employee Troubleshooting)
Four reasons employee performance drops:
1. **Communication** -- They don't know THAT you want them to do it
2. **Training** -- They don't know HOW to do it
3. **Motivation** -- They don't WANT to do it (wrong reward timing/frequency/intensity/source, aversion, life events)
4. **Circumstances** -- Something is STOPPING them (broken tools, no supplies, slow internet)

### Maker vs Manager Calendar Framework
| Dimension | Manager | Maker |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Time blocks | 30-60 min | Half-day / full-day |
| Chunks per day | 10-20 | 1-2 |
| Meeting cost | 1 work unit | 10x (destroys entire block) |
| Empty calendar | Lost opportunity | Full productivity |
| Optimal work time | During business hours | Before/after others (4am-10am) |
| Task switching | Frequent, normal | Catastrophic (Zeigarnik effect) |

## Key Principles

1. **You make more because of who THEY are, not who YOU are.** Same sales page design work: $40K/yr value for a $100K/mo company vs $4M/yr value for a $10M/mo company. Same work, 100x difference.

2. **Sell to people who don't stop buying.** Fortunes are created when we sell things customers don't stop buying. Enterprise clients get higher multiples because they afford more, deploy resources, and continue buying.

3. **Quality > Quantity on lead qualification.** Every time we removed qualification steps, lead volume increased but we made less money. Merging marketing and sales into one acquisition department solved this.

4. **"I would rather pay $5,000 to acquire $45,000 than pay $1,000 to acquire $5,000."** Even though it costs 5x more. Knowing your ideal buyer journey forces patience.

5. **Get Flow. Monetize Flow. Then Add Friction.** Generate demand first, figure out how to make money on it second, add qualification friction third. Too many people put the cart before the horse.

6. **Free is the most powerful offer of all time and will never expire.** The "penny gap" (Dan Ariely): 9x more people take a free Hershey kiss than one sold for a penny.

7. **Free brings broke people is a MYTH.** Four independent split tests of free vs premium (10 markets each): close rate was THE SAME. But free decreased lead costs by 5x or more.

8. **If I only had one offer to make to convert or my family would be killed, it would be a free offer.** I'd rather wade through crappy leads than look at an empty calendar.

9. **LTGP is the arms race of business.** You can only get CAC to zero, but LTGP can go infinitely high. The business that makes its customer most valuable wins.

10. **A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow.** Getting customers to spend more money faster is crucial for scaling any bootstrapped business.

11. **It never costs "too much" or "too little" to acquire customers. It just costs what it costs.** It's up to us to design our business to make more money from the same customers.

12. **The more time they spend with you, the more money they'll spend with you.** Rule to sell by.

13. **Everyone is replaceable, especially me.** If you want it done right, get someone to spend all their time doing it.

14. **You get rich from what you make. You become wealthy from what you own.** A business that runs without you is worth something to someone else.

15. **Customers are pre-loaded bags of money who pay for themselves and the next customer.** This is forced viral growth.

## Formulas & Equations

### CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)
**Formula:** Total costs (advertising + payroll + creative + software + commissions + salaries) / Number of customers acquired

**Three examples:**
- Outreach: ($3,000 emailer + $200 software + $800 commissions) / 8 sales = **$500 CAC**
- Content: ($10,000 media payroll + $1,000 commissions) / 10 customers = **$1,100 CAC**
- Paid Ads: ($4,000 media buyer + $20,000 ad spend + $1,000 software + $10,000 commissions) / 10 customers = **$3,500 CAC**

### LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)
**Step 1: Gross Profit**
- Product: $100 price - $20 costs = $80 GP (80% gross margin)
- Service: 10 clients x $3,000/mo = $30,000 revenue. $6,000 rep cost. GP = $24,000. Per customer = $2,400.

**Step 2: Average transactions or churn**
- Physical products: Export data, average transaction count (e.g., 4)
- Recurring: Churn = people who left / original amount (e.g., 5/100 = 5%)
  - Note: New sign-ups do NOT affect churn calculation

**Step 3: Calculate LTGP**
- Physical: GP x avg transactions = LTGP → $80 x 4 = **$360 LTGP**
- Recurring: GP / Churn = LTGP → $2,400 / 5% = **$48,000 LTGP**

### Payback Period (PPD)
- PPD = when GP > CAC (the time it takes to break even on acquisition cost)
- Example: $50/mo GP, $100 CAC → $50 day 1, $50 day 31 → **PPD = 31 days**

### 30D Cash (Alex's Most Prized Metric)
- Amount of gross profit extracted from a new customer in their first 30 days
- Critical because 30 days = interest-free financing (credit cards)
- If 30D Cash > CAC → free customers using other people's money (OPM)
- Max out credit card → acquire customers → pay off balance with 30D Cash → repeat

### CFA Level 3 Growth Table (GP = 2x CAC, all profit reinvested)
| Month | New Customers | Total Customers |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1 | 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2 | 3 |
| 3 | 4 | 7 |
| 4 | 8 | 15 |
| 5 | 16 | 31 |
| 6 | 32 | 63 |
| 7 | 64 | 127 |
| 8 | 128 | 256 |
| 9 | 256 | 511 |
| 10 | 512 | 1,023 |
| 11 | 1,024 | 2,047 |
| 12 | 2,048 | 4,095 |

Only spent $1 out of pocket. Rest = "house money."

### Premium vs Free Offer Math
- Business #1: $100/sale, $50 profit, needs 190 sales = $9,500 profit
- Business #2: $10,000/sale, $9,500 profit, closes 5% of same 190 → 9.5 sales = $90,250 profit
- **Result: 9.5x more profitable with premium offer**
- But: premium requires better copy, sales skills, and understanding of avatar

### Freemium CAC Math
- Cost per free customer: $0.05/mo
- Upgrade rate: 1%
- CAC = $0.05 / 0.01 = **$5/mo**
- Need avg revenue per paid user > $15/mo to be profitable

### Employee ROI Calculation
- Total Payroll / Total Engaged Leads = Cost per engaged lead
- Example: $100,000 payroll / 1,000 leads = $100/lead
- If 1/10 engaged leads buy → CAC = $1,000
- If LTGP = $4,000 → LTGP:CAC = **4:1**
- Acquisition.com example: $100K/mo team → 30K leads/mo = $3.33/lead. No paid ads.

## Step-by-Step Processes

### Your First Avatar Process
1. Survey customers (demographics, business stats, aspirations, buying process)
2. Sort by biggest spenders/longest stayers -- focus top 20%
3. Find 3-5 common qualifiers
4. Execute: change all advertising to speak to new avatar + re-engineer sales process based on how best customers bought

### Gym Launch Avatar Findings
- **Demographics:** Right-leaning/conservative, married, 25-45, male, gym owner, US-based
- **Business Requirements:** Signed lease, 1+ employees, $10K+/mo revenue, 30+ existing clients
- **Aspirations:** $1M+ gym, work less, open more locations
- **Buying Reasons:** Not enough leads, "bad market," bad pricing, can't find employees
- **Key Finding:** 78% of top customers consumed AT LEAST TWO pieces of long-form content before purchasing

### Five Friction Types (to qualify free leads)
1. **Increased Qualifications** -- "Must be over 25, employed, homeowner"
2. **Increased Information Requirements** -- 20-question application, cell phone, income questions
3. **Increased Number of Steps** -- Multi-step forms, certifications, video watching
4. **Forced Consumption** -- 40-minute video before CTA appears; between steps (e.g., between meetings)
5. **Advertisement Length** -- Longer ads/copy = fewer but higher-quality clicks

### Four Ways to Display Discounts
1. **Percentage Off:** "87% off first visit"
2. **Absolute Amount Off:** "$181 Off First Week" (normally $210)
3. **Relative Equivalent Off:** "Save a steak dinner" or "Less than going out to lunch"
4. **Simply the Discounted Price:** "$29 New Client Special"

### Advanced Offer Stacking -- Weight Loss Flow
**Sale #1: Service Sale (Up Front Cash)**
- High-ticket solution first → half down with payment plan → quarter down with higher payments → shorter duration → free trial with card on file → free nutrition orientation (if all nos)

**Sale #2: Physical Products Sale (Upsell)**
- 3-month supplement bundle → 1-month supply on subscription → essentials only → 1-2 must-have products → meal prep affiliate upsell

**Sale #3: More Services (Continuity)**
- Feedback meeting → sell long-term membership (high ticket prepayment → downsell to continuity)
- If unhappy: credit first program cost toward VIP program (Rollover Upsell)

**Sale #4: Prepay or Last Chance (Up Front Cash/Downsell)**
- Get uncommitted people on continuity. Upsell committed people on prepay discount.

### Free Presentation Benchmarks
| Format | Duration | Conversion Benchmark |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| Free Dinner | 60-90 min | 25% lower offer, 33% of those take higher offer |
| Free Masterclass | 90 min | 10% of attendees when offer presented |
| Free 5-Day Challenge | 4-5 days, 60-90 min each | 2-5% of signups |
| Free 3-Day Summit | 3 full days | 10% of final day attendees |
| Free Strategy Call | 30-60 min (step 1 of 2-step) | 25% of those who make it to second call |

### BANT Qualification (Strategy Calls)
- **B**udget -- Can they afford it?
- **A**uthority -- Can they make the buying decision?
- **N**eed -- Do they want what you're selling?
- **T**iming -- Do they want to start in your timeframe?

### Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate (3Ds Training)
1. **Document:** Write checklist of exact steps as you do the task. Record yourself. Test: can YOU do an A+ job following only these instructions?
2. **Demonstrate:** Walk them through checklist step by step. Adjust for confusion points.
3. **Duplicate:** They follow the checklist while you observe. Fix checklist until output matches.

### Employee Meeting Cadence
- **1x per week:** 30-45 min coaching/feedback/praising session
- **5-10x per week:** Short daily huddles (few minutes each) -- one at shift start (expectations/goals), one at shift end (report/praise)
- Total: 6-11 touchpoints per week per lead-getting employee

## Case Studies & Examples

### Vista Equity Approach
When considering acquiring a company, they analyze current customers. Score by who stays longest and pays most. If underserved valuable customers exist, they buy. Then cut channels bringing low-value customers. Double down on channels bringing best ones. Pareto on steroids: 20% bring 80% of revenue. Replace 80% with high spenders = 5x growth.

### Gym Launch vs Competitor (70x Profit Difference)
Same vertical, same number of total sales. Competitor made 70x less profit. Why: they accepted anyone with a pulse and a credit card. High churn, high acquisition cost, low retention, generic advice. Gym Launch: selectively pursued highest-value customers, ignored all others. Higher retention, premium pricing, repeat business. LTGP: $45K+ vs $6-8K.

### Alex's First Free Offer (33 Gyms)
"I'll fly out, spend my own money on marketing, work your leads, close them. I get the cash I collect, you get the customers free. I'll show you supplements, nutrition, memberships." Did 33 gyms in 18 months. Made first million outside of gyms. Started with a free offer.

### Lemonade Stand -- Normal vs Wonderful Business
**Normal:** $20 CAC, 2-month payback, $40 LTGP. From -$20 to breakeven at day 90. Hard to grow.
**Wonderful:** $1 CAC, 7-day payback. Spend $1, make $8 in 7 days. Reinvest $7 → 7 new customers. By day 60: $225+ in gross profit from a single $1 investment.

### Gym Launch Scaling Numbers
- Software ALAN: $0 to $1.7M/mo in 6 months
- Consulting business: $215K/mo to $1.8M/mo in 8 months
- E-commerce: $80K/mo to $1.5M/mo in 6 months
- First year Gym Launch: $100 back for every $1 spent (100x ROAS)
- $100,000 spend → $10M returns

### Free Car Wash (COVID-era Pick Your Price)
Austin, Texas lockdown. Girl waving "Free Car Wash" sign. Standard wash = free but accepting donations. Pricing board with bonus tiers ($30 = wax, $67 = hand buff, $99+ = full interior). Average ticket: ~$99 -- more than normal automated wash. Applied to gyms during COVID with great success.

### The Opener (Billion Dollar Gym Franchise)
Every location opened with 400+ recurring members or they don't open. 14-day trial into lifetime "founding member" discount. Only offered before opening (urgency + scarcity). 80% sign up after trial. Most stick because they don't want to lose their lifetime discount.

### Art Gallery Pick-Your-Price
Every piece had a price RANGE. "This painting is between $149 and $299." Most people pay more than halfway because they don't want to seem cheap or unsupportive.

### Weight Loss Clinic Chain (200+ locations)
$5/mo weight loss services for a year IF client uses their supplements, bars, shakes, and meal program. Guarantee contingent on continued usage. Gave service away because they made so much on consumable products. People more willing to pay for products than service from white-coated medical professionals.

### $5,000 Setup + $267/mo Weight Loss Coaching
Multi-million dollar online business. Average client lifespan: 2+ years (vs normal 4 months). Massive up front sum gets client invested. If they leave and come back, pay setup again. Sunk Cost Fallacy in action.

### Cold Outreach Bottleneck Story
Sales director said they'd hit goal next quarter (missed twice). Real issue: losing a rep every 4 weeks. HR said below industry average churn. But hiring = 1 out of 4 candidates, only 1 qualified per 10 screening interviews = 40 interviews for one hire. Solution: group interviews, push everyone with work ethic and basic social skills to sales. Within 6 weeks: hiring outpaced churn. Cold outreach doubled.

## Action Items / Tactical Advice

### Avatar & Customer Selection
1. **Survey your existing customers immediately.** Demographics, business stats, aspirations, buying process. Focus on top 20% by spend and tenure.
2. **Find 3-5 common qualifiers** among your best customers. Make those your advertising requirements.
3. **78% of Gym Launch top customers consumed 2+ long-form content pieces before purchasing.** Force this in your buyer journey.
4. **Spell out requirements in ads and pages.** Repel bad customers, attract good ones.
5. **Stop selling anyone who doesn't meet ideal customer requirements.** Seriously, stop it.
6. **If you have no customers, start with the industry you know most about.** Most inside knowledge from friends, family, past jobs.

### Pricing & Monetization
7. **Massive discounts (50%+) drive real behavior.** Marginal discounts (5-25%) just cut into margin.
8. **Discount a PIECE of your offer, not the whole thing.** It's a lead gen tool, not your business model.
9. **Use discounts to get card on file** -- makes future upsells frictionless ("use the card on file?").
10. **The two-step sale is probably the biggest benefit of discounts.** Small purchase → card on file → show up → big upsell.
11. **Add steps as price and complexity increase.** More complex/expensive = more time with prospect needed.
12. **You can charge for "made-up" fees** -- pick name, pick price, pick "reason why," start charging/discounting/waiving.
13. **The higher the one-time startup fee, the lower the churn.** $100 signup + $10/mo = almost zero churn. $19 down + $19/mo = high churn.

### Offer Design
14. **Layer offers: Attract → Up Front Cash → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity → Continuity #2 → Up Front Cash #2.** Repeat the cycle.
15. **Figure out adjacent customer needs you're NOT monetizing.** Create affiliate relationships or add low-ops upsells.
16. **Affiliate commissions add up massively.** Alex made $3M+ just in affiliate commissions. Pure profit to bottom line.
17. **Free onboarding call = opportunity to ensure customers click all affiliate links.** Commissions paid for the entire team.
18. **If someone says no to services, offer free orientation → sell products.** Products from people who said no to services = 50% more than regular clients.
19. **NOTE: If someone does not buy the upsell, they are unlikely to stay.** Product upsell take rate is the greatest predictor of back-end conversion. It's a must-have.

### Presentation-Based Selling
20. **Amount of time educating consumer is directly related to price and trust needed.** Toy plane = 20 feet runway. 747 = 2 miles.
21. **Structure presentations around the three core limiting beliefs:** easy results, best results, status/approval.
22. **Free 5-day challenges:** Convert 2-5% of signups. Four live 60-90 min presentations Mon-Thu, offer on Friday. Each day breaks one core belief.
23. **Follow-up (email, SMS, participation-based value) increases engagement, show rates, and conversion.**

### Employee Management
24. **Getting employees = getting customers.** Same process, different words. Use the Core Four (warm outreach, cold outreach, paid ads, content) for hiring too.
25. **Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate.** Make checklist → walk them through → they do it while you watch. Fix checklist until it works.
26. **Meet lead-getting employees 6-11 times per week.** One 30-45 min coaching session + daily huddles (beginning and end of shift, few minutes each).
27. **If performance drops, diagnose:** Communication → Training → Motivation → Circumstances. Don't fire until you've checked all four.
28. **Best feedback question (stolen from Leila):** "Over the last [time], your [task] has changed from the norm. What do you think has gotten in the way and how can I help?"
29. **Focus on employee's ability to follow directions, not results.** If they follow directions and get wrong result, it's the directions' fault. You control that.
30. **If CAC > 3x industry average:** Do engaged leads have the problem and the money? No = advertising problem. Yes but not buying = sales problem. Yes and buying but not enough = advertising problem. Don't fire sales if it's advertising and vice versa.

### Maker/Manager Time Management
31. **Schedule meetings from back to front** (latest possible in the day) to preserve maker blocks.
32. **First half of day = maker work. Second half = manager work.** Meetings after 1pm.
33. **Set expectations for slow responses during maker blocks.** People adapt if told ahead of time.
34. **Organizations: consider mandated "quiet" chunks** where teams cannot message or meet.
35. **If meeting breaks your maker block, swap to manager calendar style** and batch all admin tasks into the broken block.
